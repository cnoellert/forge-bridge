## The frame is right; the altitude claim is right; two of the supporting struts are softer than DT drew them

DT's central move — *we have been prototyping a shipped contract; Phase X is unframed the measurement pass for bridge's reference-resolution quality* — is correct and I endorse it without reservation. The constitution's §6 ("Resolution success, resolution failure, and resolution ambiguity MUST be representable explicitly. A capability MUST surface unresolved or ambiguous references as typed outcomes rather than silently falling back", ADR-000:142-144) is the *exact same concern* the instrument was built around: S4's whole reason for existence is that an analyzer seeing only one failure mode "biases the build/don't-build ruling toward don't-build" (`_analysis.py:4-7`) — i.e. it refuses to let resolution fail silently. That is constitutional §6, one altitude down. The vocabulary alignment is real and worth recording.

But two of DT's struts carry more weight than the files support, and both happen to be the struts that, if accepted uncritically, would either (a) inflate Phase X's federation importance enough to tempt urgency into the measurement, or (b) commit a contract shape the S4 code cannot currently produce. I redline both below, then resolve the asks in a way that holds the measure-first line.

## Redline of DT's three positions

### Position 1 — "Do NOT re-scope Phase X." → **ENDORSE, unqualified.**

This is correct and it is the most important of the three. The reframe is vocabulary alignment, not a new mission; ruling (iii) holds; the instrument was right and now we know its output has a name and a second consumer. The Capture-Model Spec §0 already fences this ("It is NOT a re-scope of Phase X (ruling iii)", line 11) and the Q3 doc §4 fences it again ("Not a re-scope of Phase X (ruling iii)", line 54). DT's position is consistent with both. Nothing to sharpen — keep it exactly as stated, and treat it as the governing constraint the other two positions must not quietly violate.

### Position 2 — "If Q3's R clears BUILD, the resolver emits `ReferenceResolution`." → **ENDORSE the direction, SHARPEN the cost claim. It is NOT quite "costs us nothing now."**

The *direction* is right: when the resolver is built, it should emit the shipped contract, not a parallel bridge-internal shape — that forecloses a vocabulary migration later, and the contract genuinely exists (`references.py:34-43`, status/reference/locations/candidates/reason_code/message all present). Recording that as the build-target in spec §8 is good forward-pointing.

But DT's phrase "**This costs us nothing now**" (SURFACING line 44) is the soft scope-creep to watch. Committing to a contract *output shape* before the measurement runs is not literally free, for two reasons grounded in the code:

1. **The contract has a shape the current S4 analysis cannot fill — specifically the `ambiguous` / `candidates[]` arm** (see the §-below mismatch catch). If §8 of the spec says "S4 maps 1:1 onto `ReferenceResolution`," that *reads* as a measurement claim ("we measure all three statuses") when it is actually an aspiration ("we would, if we built the disambiguation path the code doesn't have"). The risk is not migration cost — it's that a reader of §8 *believes the instrument already measures ambiguity* and ranks the BUILD decision against a richer signal than the corpus actually contains. That is the well-formedness-precedes-content sin in federation costume: a populated-looking cell that is actually aspiration.

2. **"Emits `ReferenceResolution`" is a statement about the *resolver*, which is gated behind R≥0.50.** Folding the contract commitment into spec §8 *now* is fine as a forward-pointer; it becomes scope-creep the moment it's read as a design obligation that pulls resolver-shaping work forward of the measurement. Keep it explicitly subordinate to the gate: the resolver's output contract is decided, the resolver's *existence* is not.

**Sharpened position:** Endorse "the resolver, IF built, emits `ReferenceResolution`." Strike or footnote "costs us nothing now" — the honest version is "*the output vocabulary costs nothing to commit; the contract's `ambiguous` arm is unmeasured today and §8 must say so, or it overstates the instrument.*" Cheap is not free, and the gap is exactly in the arm the contract makes load-bearing (§6 names ambiguity a first-class outcome).

### Position 3 — "The §2.3 op→type map should become pipeline-declared capability metadata, not hook-source reverse-engineering." → **ENDORSE strongly, and it is the most architecturally durable of the three.**

This is the best catch DT made and I want it sharpened *upward*, not softened. It is grounded dead-on: ADR-000 §3 ("Discovery Over Privileged Coupling") literally prescribes the test — "*Could this information be declared by the capability instead? If yes, prefer declaration over Bridge-side special-case logic*" (ADR-000:110-112). The Capture-Model Spec's own provenance line admits the violation in passing: §2.3 is "mechanically derived from the production hooks at `~/GitHub/forge-pipeline/flame_hooks/forge_tools/`" (spec line 4) — bridge reading a sibling's hook internals to reconstruct "forge_rename requires a selected PySequence" is precisely the no-sibling-internals boundary (ADR-000:89-90, §3). DT naming it "an interim that violates the ADR-000 boundary, not entrenched" is the correct governance move.

**One sharpening (a real subtlety the §2.4 refinement surfaces that DT's clean framing flattens):** the thing pipeline would declare is **not a simple op→type map.** Spec §2.4 establishes that the guard is heterogeneous — nominal `isinstance`, behavioral `hasattr(s, "record_in")`, and cardinality `len(segs)==1` (spec lines 60-65). So "pipeline declares its referent requirements" means pipeline declares a **predicate descriptor**, not a type name. `forge_switch_grade`'s requirement is "a selection where `hasattr(item, 'record_in')`", which does not reduce to a `PySequence`-style type string. DT's "extending `CapabilityDeclaration`" is right; what gets declared is richer than the §2.3 table's middle column suggests, and the declaration schema has to carry predicate semantics (or pipeline pre-resolves the predicate and declares the *outcome* contract, i.e. emits the satisfying selection itself). This matters because it tells you **where the resolver lives**: if pipeline declares only the predicate, bridge still reconstructs it (the reconstructability problem from §4 moves but does not vanish); if pipeline declares the *resolved referent*, the §4 reconstructability unknown dissolves entirely and the resolver thins toward nothing on bridge's side. That is a Creative-lane question (the surfacing doc's Creative ask (b) already gestures at it) — but the §2.4 heterogeneity is the reason it's a real fork, not a formality.

## The sharpest catch — the S4→`ReferenceResolution` table is NOT 1:1; DT smoothed two seams

I read `_analysis.py` against DT's mapping table (SURFACING lines 27-35) and the spec §8 table (lines 138-140). Two mismatches, one material:

**(1) MATERIAL — there is no `ambiguous` / `candidates` path in the code.** S4's auto-flagger emits exactly two modes: `unresolved_reference` and `wrong_resolution` (`_analysis.py:177, 187`; docstring lines 11-21). There is **no third mode for "≥2 selections, can't disambiguate."** Worse, the code is structurally incapable of representing it today: `_selected_value` returns the *first* matching typed object and stops (`_analysis.py:136-139`), and the comparison is a scalar `compiled != focus` (`:185`). A two-selection world-state would silently pick one referent and then classify the record as `wrong_resolution` or correct — it could never surface as ambiguity. So DT's table row "≥2 selections, can't disambiguate → `status="ambiguous"` + `candidates`" (SURFACING line 32) and the identical claim in spec §8 ("multiple-selection-can't-disambiguate → `ambiguous` + `candidates`", spec line 140) map a contract arm onto **code that does not exist and cannot currently produce it.** This is the federation-costume version of well-formedness-precedes-content: the `ambiguous` cell is aspiration presented as a populated mapping. The contract's own §6 makes ambiguity first-class (ADR-000:142-144), which makes the gap *more* conspicuous, not less — the one arm the constitution most insists on is the one S4 doesn't measure.

**(2) MINOR but real — `failure_class` string drift.** DT's table and spec §8 use `failure_class="wrong_referent"` (SURFACING line 30; spec §8 line 140; Q3 §1 line 16). The *auto-flag mode* in the code is `wrong_resolution` (`_analysis.py:13, 186, 192`). The *authored* `failure_class` is a free-form authored string (`author_analysis(..., failure_class=...)`, `:204`) — it is not constrained to match the mode vocabulary, and the Q3 doc independently asserts the authored taxonomy is `{wrong_referent, unresolved_reference}` (Q3 §1 line 16). So there are two parallel taxonomies — auto-flag `mode` (`wrong_resolution`/`unresolved_reference`) and authored `failure_class` (`wrong_referent`/`unresolved_reference`) — and they agree on one term and diverge on the other. Not a bug (authoring is deliberately a separate judgement layer, `:209-213`), but DT's table reads as if there's one vocabulary. When §8 reaches the contract, this needs a deliberate crosswalk, not an implied identity.

**What I am NOT saying:** this does not invalidate the reframe or Position 2's direction. It sharpens the cost claim: the mapping is 1:1 on the two arms S4 actually measures (`resolved` / `unresolved`, plus `resolved`-but-wrong as the ratify-caught case) and **aspirational on the third** (`ambiguous`). Spec §8 should be edited to say so explicitly — "S4 measures resolved/unresolved/resolved-but-wrong today; `ambiguous`+`candidates` is a contract arm the instrument does not yet exercise (single-referent `_selected_value`, scalar comparison) — building it is part of the gated resolver work, not a current measurement." That one edit converts an overstated 1:1 into an honest forward-pointer and is the concrete redline I'd ask DT to take.

## The federation-dependency claim is INFLATED — Phase 5 is a different seam than deictic resolution

DT's bottleneck argument (SURFACING lines 37-39) asserts Phase 5 (Bridge Discovery) and Phase 7 (E2E Demonstrator) "gate on bridge orchestrating … Bridge cannot plan-and-invoke until it resolves the artist's references" and concludes "composition *through* bridge is blocked on our reference-resolution maturity." I read FEDERATION-PROOF-SEQUENCE.md Phase 5 carefully and this overclaims.

**Phase 5 is capability-family discovery and routing, not deictic referent resolution.** Its work items are "Discover Vision and Pipeline via entry points · Build capability snapshots from declarations · **Route by capability family, not package identity**" and its success criterion is verbatim: *"Bridge asks: Which declared capability satisfies this step? not: Which special package do I call?"* (FEDERATION-PROOF-SEQUENCE.md:131-154). That is a *routing* seam — matching a step to a declared capability — and it is **orthogonal to resolving "this sequence" into a `PySequence` name.** A bridge that can route "rename step → pipeline's rename capability" without yet resolving *which* sequence the artist meant still satisfies Phase 5's criterion. Phase 7 (E2E Demonstrator, :174-187) does say "records references, events, lineage, and provenance" — references appear there — but as *recorded coordination objects*, not as a prerequisite that deictic-reference-quality must clear first.

**The accurate claim, narrowed:** reference-resolution maturity is relevant to a *high-quality* E2E demonstration (Phase 7) — an artist saying "publish this sequence" and having it flow through is a better demo than passing explicit IDs. But it is **not a gate on Phase 5**, and it is **not the blocker** on Phase 7 either — Phase 7's gate is that all the *siblings* compose (discovery + invocation + receipt reconciliation), which can be demonstrated with explicit references. DT's "the federation has been waiting on this seam" (line 39) is the inflation. The federation is waiting on Generators (Phase 6, in flight) and on bridge's discovery wiring (Phase 5, capability-routing) — neither of which is the v1.7→v1.13→Phase X deictic grind. Phase X *improves* the eventual demonstrator; it does not *unblock* the proof sequence.

**Why this redline matters and isn't pedantry:** the inflated dependency is exactly the lever that would justify hurrying the measurement — "Phase 5/7 are blocked on us, so re-rank the live re-capture up." If the dependency is real and gating, urgency has a legitimate claim. Because it is *not* gating (Phase 5 routes without it), urgency has no legitimate claim, and the measure-first discipline is preserved on its own terms rather than being overridden by a manufactured downstream bottleneck. This directly sets up Ask #2.

## Ask #1 — Re-cast the close as a "reference-resolution measurement pass"? **YES at the documentation/legibility layer; NO at the obligation layer. Re-frame, do not re-contract.**

Re-cast the *eventual close artifact* to name the federation read — Phase X's close should say, in plain terms, "this was the measurement pass for bridge's reference-resolution quality, the bridge-side instrument behind the federation's `ReferenceResolution` contract." That is legible, true, and costs nothing — it's the same altitude-correction DT surfaced, recorded where Phase 5/7 authors will find it. **Do this.**

But do **not** re-frame in a way that makes Phase X *obligated to* the contract. Three reasons it would over-couple:

1. **Phase X is a bridge-internal instrument; the contract is a federation artifact.** Binding the close to "this satisfies `ReferenceResolution`" implies a conformance obligation the instrument doesn't owe — the instrument *measures whether to build the thing that would emit the contract.* It is one layer below the contract, by its own §0 ("It is NOT the resolver"). A close that says "Phase X delivers `ReferenceResolution`" is wrong; a close that says "Phase X measures whether bridge should build a `ReferenceResolution`-emitting resolver" is right.
2. **The `ambiguous` gap (above) means a contract-framed close would overstate coverage.** If the close says "reference-resolution measurement pass" without the caveat that one of the three statuses is unmeasured, the federation reader inherits the aspiration-as-fact problem. The honest close names *which* resolution outcomes the instrument actually measured.
3. **Distinct success criteria per adjacent layer.** Phase X's native success criterion is the resolvable-delta against the Q3 threshold — a bridge-legibility/friction-removal number. The federation read is an *additional* legibility win that rides on top; it must not be laundered into the success criterion. The close reports the resolvable-delta as the verdict and *notes* the federation framing as context — it does not restate the federation read as the thing Phase X succeeded-or-failed at.

**Net:** re-cast the *prose frame* of the close to make the federation read legible to Phase 5/7 (with the `ambiguous`-unmeasured caveat). Do **not** re-cast the *obligation* — Phase X stays a measurement pass whose verdict is the Q3 delta, not a contract-conformance deliverable.

## Ask #2 — Does the federation consumer re-rank sequencing? **NO. Measure-first stays the only gate, and urgency stays deliberately OUT of the measurement. This is the discipline working, not a constraint to route around.**

The federation read does **not** re-rank the live re-capture + resolver build, for a chain of grounded reasons:

1. **The premise for re-ranking is the Phase 5/7 dependency — which I just showed is inflated** (Phase 5 is capability-routing, not deictic resolution). Remove the false bottleneck and the urgency argument loses its load-bearing claim. There is no downstream gate pulling Phase X forward.
2. **Even if the dependency were real, the blind Q3 threshold cannot be hurried without corrupting it.** The Q3 doc is explicit: "If the threshold is set after the number is known, it reverse-engineers to fit — which would void the measure-first gate" (Q3 §0). Sequencing-urgency is a softer form of the same corruption: a known downstream consumer waiting on a BUILD verdict is precisely the pressure that biases an authored `world_state_resolvable` judgement toward "resolvable" (toward BUILD). The blind precommit defends against post-hoc threshold-fitting; it does **not** defend against *authoring* bias introduced by knowing a sibling needs the build. The only defense against *that* is to keep the federation urgency out of the room while the delta is authored. **Letting Phase 5/7 re-rank the re-capture is the anti-pattern the blind threshold exists to prevent, displaced one step earlier into the authoring.**
3. **The re-capture is already correctly gated on a real dogfood moment + settled hook-deployment state** (SURFACING line 53; spec §7 step 5). Those are the right gates — they ensure the corpus reflects real pressure, not harness pressure. Federation demand is not one of those gates and adding it would substitute "a sibling wants this" for "a real operator generated this failure," which is the ecological-validity regression we've guarded twice (CR.1, TF.3a).

**The one legitimate, urgency-free move:** the federation read *does* change one thing — it raises the *value of the eventual resolver if BUILD fires*, because the same resolver serves two consumers (chat legibility + the federation demonstrator) instead of one. That is a fact worth recording in the close (it strengthens the post-BUILD investment case). But it changes nothing *before* the measurement: it does not raise the priority of running the re-capture, does not lower the BUILD threshold, and does not earn the resolver a build it didn't measure into. **Higher payoff on a BUILD verdict is not a reason to reach the verdict faster or more leniently.** Record the doubled payoff; hold it strictly downstream of the gate.

**Flagging the anti-pattern explicitly (binding):** any proposal of the form "Phase 5/7 are waiting, so let's prioritize the live re-capture / build the thinnest resolver speculatively / treat MORE-DATA as soft-BUILD because the federation wants it" is urgency leaking into the measurement. Name it and reject it. The blind threshold and the dogfood gate are the only things that move the resolver from gated to built.

## Forward-pointer / unresolved tension for the room

**One concrete redline for DT to take:** edit Capture-Model Spec §8 and the SURFACING mapping table to mark the `ambiguous` / `candidates` arm as **unmeasured-by-current-S4** (single-referent `_selected_value`, scalar comparison) rather than 1:1 — and add the `wrong_resolution`↔`wrong_referent` crosswalk note. That converts an overstated mapping into an honest forward-pointer without touching scope or the threshold.

**The unresolved tension I want the room to carry into Creative's pass:** Position 3 (pipeline-declares-the-predicate) and the §4 reconstructability unknown are the *same question wearing two hats.* If pipeline declares only a predicate descriptor, bridge still has to reconstruct the guard outcome (§4(b) reconstructability survives) and the `ambiguous` arm still has to be built on bridge's side. If pipeline declares the *resolved satisfying selection* (pre-applies its own guard, which it alone can do cleanly per §5.1's "the hook IS the guard"), then §4 reconstructability dissolves, the resolver thins toward nothing, AND `candidates[]` becomes *pipeline's* output, not bridge's reconstruction — which is the cleanest path to the contract's `ambiguous` arm. That is a where-does-the-resolver-live decision with real federation consequences, and it is Creative's lane (the surfacing doc's Creative ask (b) opened it). It does **not** need resolving before the measurement — but the room should not let the §8 "1:1 mapping" language quietly pre-decide it by implying bridge owns `candidates` generation. Hold the fork open until the re-capture says BUILD; if it says REJECT, the fork is moot.

**Measure-first is intact.** Nothing in the federation surfacing changes the gate, the threshold, or the sequence. The reframe is a legibility win recorded above the measurement, held strictly out of it.
