## 2. Gate 3 objective

### 2.1 Three inherited truths from Gate 2

Gate 3 opens on three architectural truths the Gate 2 arc
established operationally. They anchor every binding decision
§5 carries and every carrier-delta §6 introduces.

1. **Grounded operational topology outranks speculative
   semantic symmetry.** PR 9's grounding-time amendment is
   now precedent, not anecdote. Framing-time extrapolation of
   a substrate's behavior — when the substrate is described
   but unexercised — is the canonical failure mode the
   amendment-at-incarnation taxonomy's fourth variant
   corrects. Empirical grounding precedes ontological
   generalization. Gate 3 inherits this as constraint, not
   guidance.

2. **Carrier #15's third clause is active governance, not
   archival prose.** *"Cross-surface expectation semantics
   require a dedicated framing pass before implementation
   proceeds"* is governance the framing must operationally
   honor. Gate 3 becomes the first proof that a carrier can
   successfully defer architectural scope **without blocking
   forward progress** — deferral as a first-class governance
   action, not a punt. If Gate 3 advances to a working
   comparator without addressing the four §7.3 ontological
   questions, the carrier matures from constraint to
   demonstrated methodology.

3. **Gate 2 validated decomposition; Gate 3 validates
   recomposition discipline.** PR 7 decomposed provenance.
   PR 8 decomposed authored declaration from observational
   capture. PR 9 decomposed proof topology from fixture
   infrastructure. **Gate 3 is the first surface where those
   decomposed surfaces are meaningfully recomposed — and the
   first place the recomposition must demonstrably preserve
   the decomposition.** Recomposition pressure is the new
   dominant cleanup vector at Gate 3 (§4.2 enumerates seven
   canonical forms).

These three truths are not philosophical claims; they are
operational constraints derived from the Gate 2 arc's
archaeology. The framing language throughout this artifact
treats them as binding.

### 2.2 What Gate 3 ships

Gate 3 ships the **interpretive comparator surface** — the
third truth class named at Gate 2 framing §2 but explicitly
not exercised at Gate 2. Where Gate 2's three-authority-
surface partition was decomposition across distinct authority
claims (observation + dispatch provenance + authored
expectation), Gate 3 is the first place those surfaces become
meaningfully recomposed into a single derived view: a
divergence report correlating what the fixture *declared
should happen* against what arbitration *actually did*.

### 2.3 Proactive scope guardrail

**The comparator compares authored expectation records
against observed arbitration records within a single
operational arbitration surface.**

Explicit rejections of broader scope language:

- **Not** *"logical prompts."* A logical prompt is a cross-
  surface construct; expectation records are scope-local to
  one arbitration surface per the PR 9 grounding-time
  amendment. Treating fixture identity as logical-prompt
  identity implicitly answers §7.3 question 2 by stealth.
- **Not** *"semantic tasks."* Tasks are operator-domain
  vocabulary; arbitration records are arbitration-pipeline
  vocabulary. The vocabulary shift smuggles ontology that
  carrier #15 explicitly defers.
- **Not** *"cross-surface executions."* Per carrier #15 +
  §6.3, cross-surface comparison is intentionally unbound at
  Gate 3.

This scope guardrail is load-bearing wording. PR-level
documentation, test docstrings, commit message bodies, and
the comparator module's own docstring carry the guardrail
verbatim. The wording prevents implicit ontology creep before
the comparator's structured return shape (PR 10 spec) is
authored — the moment most likely to fold a broader scope
into the comparator's vocabulary if unguarded. §5.6 carries
the binding-decision form of this guardrail.

### 2.4 The truth-class table

The architectural commitment is **recomposition without
collapse**:

| Authority surface | Helper | Truth claim | Record kind | Gate of operation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Observational | `emit_divergence_capture(...)` | "I observed finalized arbitration." | observation | Gate 2 |
| Authored expectation | `emit_seed_expectation(...)` | "The fixture declared this should happen." | expectation | Gate 2 |
| Interpretive | Gate 3 comparator | "These two records diverged in the following way." | (none — derived view, not persisted record) | Gate 3 |

Three truth classes. Three authority surfaces. **Two persisted
record kinds; one derived view.** The comparator is a read-
time computation that produces a structured report. It does
not write to the corpus, does not introduce a third
`record_kind`, and does not modify either upstream surface.

The composition is the deliverable, but the **decomposition
must survive the composition**. Gate 3 demonstrates that the
three-authority-surface partition holds under recomposition
pressure. Cleanup proposals to collapse the seam are rejected
at the spec layer per carrier #17 (§6.2) + §4.2's enumeration
of seven canonical recomposition pressure forms.

## 4. Architectural delta — what Gate 3 introduces

### 4.1 Interpretive authority surface operationalized

Gate 3 introduces the third truth class as an operational
surface:

> **Interpretive authority claim:** "These two records,
> jointly, diverged in the following way."

The surface is a single function (signature finalized in
PR 10 spec) living in `forge_bridge/corpus/_compare.py`. The
function:

- accepts a `fixture_id`-indexed corpus subset (observation +
  expectation records for the same fixture identity).
- computes a structured divergence report (shape finalized in
  PR 10 spec).
- returns the report as a pure value — no side effects, no
  persistence, no logging.

**The surface is read-only by construction.** It cannot write
to the corpus, cannot mutate input records, cannot trigger
upstream emission. The protection is enforced structurally
(`_compare.py` imports neither emission helper nor any writer
primitive; §8.2 names the Layer 2 mechanism deferred to PR 10
spec).

### 4.2 Recomposition as the new dominant cleanup vector

Gate 2 validated **decomposition** across three authority
surfaces. PR 7 decomposed provenance into observation +
dispatch-context. PR 8 decomposed authored declaration from
observational capture. PR 9 decomposed proof topology from
fixture infrastructure. Each decomposition was load-bearing
in isolation.

Gate 3 is the first place those surfaces become **meaningfully
recomposed** into a single derived view. **Recomposition
pressure is the new dominant cleanup vector at Gate 3.** Once
a comparator exists, the temptation profile shifts — surfaces
that survived independent existence face a new collapse
incentive once a downstream reader joins them.

**Seven canonical pressure forms — enumerated so Gate 3
implementation has explicit rejection language available at
the moment each form surfaces:**

1. **Helper merger.** *"Just merge `emit_seed_expectation` and
   `emit_divergence_capture` into one helper — the comparator
   reads both anyway."* Rejected per member #8
   (`emit_seed_expectation` as semantics-not-topology) +
   carrier #17 (recomposition preserves authorship). The
   helpers' singular authority claims are the protection;
   merging collapses two truth classes into one helper.
2. **Schema merger.** *"Since the comparator reads both
   records anyway, the schema could merge into a single
   record_kind with optional expectation fields."* Rejected
   per carrier #17 + member #7 (companion records as truth-
   partitioning). The two-element `_KNOWN_RECORD_KINDS` set
   is structurally locked per Gate 2 close §2.4 item 3.
3. **Persistence creep.** *"The divergence report could
   persist as a third record_kind for downstream consumers."*
   Rejected per §5.4 + carrier #17 (derived view, not
   authority surface). Adding `record_kind="divergence"`
   would require a third helper + a third truth claim — the
   comparator becomes an authority surface, which violates
   its structural definition.
4. **Inline emission.** *"The expectation helper could
   capture divergence inline since the comparator is its only
   consumer."* Rejected per member #7 + member #8. Folding
   interpretive computation into authored-declaration
   emission smears the two surfaces' authority partition.
5. **Premature surface normalization.** *"Just normalize
   observation's nested `narrower.decision` to match
   expectation's flat `expected_narrow` shape — it'd
   simplify the comparator."* Rejected per the proactive
   scope guardrail (§2.3) + the four inherited §7.3
   ontological questions remaining unbound (§5.2). **Surface
   differences ARE the comparator's input ontology;
   normalizing them before the comparator authors
   interpretation collapses the authority partition the
   comparator is supposed to preserve.**
6. **Speculative fixture-semantics widening.** *"Just add
   chain-step fixture variants to the corpus now — they'll
   be ready when chain-step seeding lands."* Rejected per
   carrier #15's third clause + PR 9 close §2.1 (the three
   fixtures are load-bearing archaeology; new fixtures
   inside Gate 3 scope are chat-handler-surface fixtures
   only). This is the speculative-extrapolation hazard
   inherited truth #1 (§2.1) explicitly governs against.
7. **Walker abstraction.** *"Just abstract the three Layer 2
   walkers into a generic AST-walking helper — they share
   mechanics."* Rejected per Gate 2 close §1.6 + §2.4 item 5
   + PR 9 spec §4.6 + §7. Shared AST mechanics do not imply
   shared ontology; collapsing three ontologies into one
   rejection surface erodes three distinct protections.

Each pressure form attacks a different protection. Carrier
#17 + the cleanup-pressure-resistance class members #7 + #8
+ #9 + the three-walker partition together protect against
all seven. **The enumeration is not exhaustive.** It names
the pressure forms already visible at framing time from
architectural inheritance. New pressure forms — and the
corresponding new class members — are expected to surface
as Gate 3 implementation proceeds. The discipline is to
register members at their per-PR close, not to author them
speculatively at framing time. §7 item 13 carries the
non-acquisition commitment on speculative class-member
authoring.

### 4.3 Comparator as pure-functional read surface

The comparator's architectural shape is **a function, not a
subsystem**. Structurally:

- Single module: `forge_bridge/corpus/_compare.py`.
- Single function (name finalized in PR 10 spec) accepting
  records, returning a divergence report.
- No state, no class hierarchy, no plugin surface, no
  registry, no configuration object.
- Imports are read-only (reader surfaces, schema constants if
  needed). No emission, no persistence.

This shape is itself a **corroboration substrate** for the
candidate carrier #16 (*"Reliability work proves topology,
not infrastructure"*). The operational governance Gate 3
acts on is the **Gate-3-LOCAL form**: *"Gate 3 proves
topology, not infrastructure"* (§6.1). A class hierarchy with
strategies, a plugin registry, or a configuration surface
would be **infrastructure** — the canonical scope-creep the
Gate-3-LOCAL form rejects. The comparator is **topology**:
which surfaces compose, in which direction, with which
authority partition preserved.

If Gate 3 implementation feels structural pressure toward
infrastructure-shaped designs (strategy pattern for
comparison algorithms; registry for divergence types; etc.),
the pressure is the canonical Gate-3-LOCAL signal. The
discipline is to collapse the design back to a function with
a structured return value and surface the pressure as a
class member candidate at close, not to accommodate the
pressure. Successful collapse-under-pressure is the
operational evidence Gate 3 close evaluates when deciding
whether to promote candidate carrier #16 to active.

### 4.4 Path B — explicit unbinding of cross-surface semantics

Per Gate 2 close §2.3, Path B scopes Gate 3 comparator
semantics to the only empirically grounded arbitration surface
currently operationalized: the chat-handler surface
(consumed end-to-end through the PR 9 three-fixture corpus).

**Path B is not within-surface-only-by-assumption.** The
scoping is operational and grounded:

- Carrier #15 governs scope: "chat-handler-only seeding;
  chain-step deferred; cross-surface expectation semantics
  require a dedicated framing pass before implementation
  proceeds."
- PR 9 §4.7 amendment canonicalized chat-handler-surface
  narrowing topology as structurally distinct from chain-step
  zero-match topology. Cross-surface arbitration outcomes are
  empirically known to differ.
- No chain-step seeding has been operationalized. Designing
  cross-surface comparator semantics without an empirically
  grounded second surface would repeat the framing-time
  extrapolation hazard PR 9 §4.7 corrected.

**Cross-surface semantics remain explicitly unbound, not
implicitly rejected** (§6.3). Gate 3's scoping preserves
future maneuverability: when chain-step seeding surfaces
operationally, a dedicated framing pass (per carrier #15's
third clause) addresses the four §7.3 ontological questions
with concrete grounding. Gate 3 does not commit to specific
cross-surface partition strategies; it explicitly defers them.

This is **deferral as a first-class governance action, not a
punt.** The methodology generalizes: when a carrier exists
encoding deferred scope, honoring the governance operationally
*is* the architectural move. Treating the carrier as
ornamental — addressing the deferred scope speculatively
without operational grounding — erodes the architecture.

## 5. Binding decisions

### 5.1 Path B locked

Gate 3 scopes comparator semantics to within-surface
comparison only, against the chat-handler arbitration surface
empirically grounded by PR 9's three-fixture corpus. The four
§7.3 ontological questions (Gate 2 close §2.3) remain
explicitly unbound; their resolution is a future-gate
deliverable that surfaces with chain-step seeding or other
cross-surface operational grounding.

**Operational consequences:**

- Comparator's `fixture_id` join semantics target a single
  arbitration surface per fixture. The question "does
  `fixture_id` identify a logical prompt or a specific
  arbitration surface" is **answered for Gate 3 scope only**:
  fixture identity within chat-handler scope, no cross-surface
  semantics implied. When chain-step seeding surfaces, the
  question reopens.
- Comparator output shape does not encode surface identity.
  Adding a surface discriminator to divergence reports is a
  future-gate decision contingent on operational need.
- The PR 9 fixture corpus is the canonical seed input set.
  Additional fixtures within Gate 3 scope (PR 11+) are
  chat-handler-surface fixtures. Cross-surface fixtures are
  rejected at the spec layer per carrier #15.

### 5.2 The four §7.3 ontological questions remain unbound

Gate 2 close §2.3 names four questions that Gate 3 framing
must address if it touches cross-surface fixture identity.
**Gate 3 framing does not touch cross-surface fixture
identity.** The questions remain unbound:

1. Does one expectation target one observation surface or
   multiple? — **unbound.** Gate 3 comparator targets the
   chat-handler surface; Gate-X framing addresses the
   multi-surface case.
2. Does `fixture_id` identify a logical prompt or a specific
   arbitration surface? — **unbound.** Gate 3 treats fixture
   identity as scope-local to chat-handler.
3. Is cross-surface divergence meaningful or noise? —
   **unbound.** Gate 3 does not produce cross-surface
   divergences.
4. Does Gate 4 compare within surfaces or across them? —
   **unbound for cross-surface case.** Gate 3 comparator
   compares within the chat-handler surface; the cross-surface
   case opens with future cross-surface framing.

The unbinding is **explicit and operational**. Gate 3
implementation does not encode answers to these questions
implicitly through comparator design. Any PR-level decision
that would commit one of the questions to a specific answer
without a dedicated framing pass is rejected at the spec
layer.

### 5.3 Comparator is pure-functional and returns structured divergence

The comparator is a single function returning a structured
divergence report as a pure value. Strong rejection of
alternative shapes:

| Alternative | Status | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Subsystem with strategies/plugins | rejected | Gate-3-LOCAL governance ("Gate 3 proves topology, not infrastructure") + candidate carrier #16 |
| Returns `None`; writes to corpus | rejected | §5.4 + carrier #17 |
| Streaming/iterator output | rejected | structural complexity without operational need; defer until pressure surfaces |
| Async function | rejected | comparator is computation over loaded records; no I/O concurrency benefit |
| Class with state | rejected | function with structured return is the minimum-topology shape |
| Side-effect logging | rejected at default | optional caller-provided logger acceptable in PR 10 spec; default is silent |

**The signature, return shape, and module placement are
finalized in PR 10 spec.** Framing locks the architectural
posture (pure-functional, structured return, no persistence,
single module) but defers the symbolic details.

### 5.4 Comparator persists nothing

The comparator produces a derived view. **No third
`record_kind`.** No fourth member added to `KNOWN_SOURCE_VALUES`.
No persistence side effect of any kind from the comparator
itself.

**Rejected:**

- Persisting divergence reports as `record_kind="divergence"`
  records. (Carrier #17 — the join is a derived view, not an
  authority surface.)
- Mutating observation or expectation records to add a
  `compared_at` timestamp or similar provenance. (Member #7
  protection — companion records as truth-partitioning.)
- Writing a sidecar artifact next to the JSONL corpus.
  (Member #8 + member #9 protections — orchestration vs.
  data-discipline; the comparator is neither orchestrator nor
  data declaration.)

The comparator's output is a Python value. Whether the caller
persists, prints, asserts on, or discards the report is the
caller's responsibility. The comparator is structurally
incapable of opinion on the caller's downstream choice.
