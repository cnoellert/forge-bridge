## 2. Gate 4 objective

### 2.1 Three inherited truths from Gate 3

Gate 4 opens on three architectural truths the Gate 3 arc
established operationally. They anchor every binding decision
§5 carries, every architectural-delta §4 introduces, and the
two-placement methodology structure §6 names.

1. **Reliability work proves topology, not infrastructure.**
   This is now active carrier #16 (promoted at Gate 3 close
   §1.6 against three explicit criteria: ≥4 surfaces with
   12-cumulative travel evidence; zero counter-examples; the
   generalized form holds without exception language). The
   form governs ALL Gate 4 reliability work at and beyond
   this framing. Gate 4 commitments that would constitute
   *inventing topology* (designing semantics without
   empirical grounding) rather than *proving topology*
   (operationally validating decisions already made) are
   rejected at the framing layer — a categorical constraint,
   not a preference.

2. **Cleanup-pressure-resistance is now named methodology.**
   The 10-member class promoted at Gate 3 close §1.5 — five-
   phase populating (PR 6 + PR 7 + PR 8 + PR 9 + PR 10) +
   operational enforcement at every member's protection
   placement — registers in `SEED-RELIABILITY-PHASE-
   METHODOLOGY-V1.6+.md` as named methodology. Future-phase
   protections of new class member candidates evaluate against
   the same four-criterion standard (genuine cleanup pressure
   / prevented real erosion / recurred independently / required
   active enforcement). Gate 4 inherits the discipline as
   constraint on amendment behavior, not advisory.

3. **Path B is causal continuation, not apologetic deferral.**
   Gate 3's Path B lock (framing §5.1) preserved cross-surface
   semantics as intentionally unbound. The three-PR
   architectural sufficiency escalation (PR 9: 0 prod mods →
   PR 10: 1 new file → PR 11: 0 prod mods) + PR 11's
   zero-incarnation-amendments cleanest arc + the §5.3
   candidate methodology two-instance ABSENCE corroboration
   together produce evidence stronger than Gate 3 framing
   anticipated. The evidence reclassifies Path B at Gate 4:
   from *"the carrier defers; we honor the carrier"* to
   *"the architecture's empirical grounding has not changed;
   speculating past the currently grounded surface would
   constitute topology invention, not topology proof."*
   The reclassification is operational (§4.3 + §5.1 binding),
   not stylistic.

These three truths are not philosophical claims; they are
operational constraints derived from the Gate 3 arc's
archaeology. The framing language throughout this artifact
treats them as binding.

### 2.2 What Gate 4 ships

Gate 4 ships **divergence-shape robustness** — the operational
exercise of the validated comparator (PR 10) + recomposition
arc (PR 11) under three architecturally distinct divergence
pressure forms. Where Gate 3 validated *can the recomposition
arc run end-to-end?*, Gate 4 validates the next layer of the
same question:

> **Does the topology preserve authored meaning under
> increasingly adversarial divergence forms?**

Each divergence dimension pressures a different topology
layer:

| Dimension | Pressures | Validates |
|---|---|---|
| **Ordering divergence** | Compare-as-persisted discipline (PR 10 §4.2 binding behavioral commitment) | The comparator preserves authored sequence exactly as persisted; no canonicalization / sorting / repair |
| **Partial-narrow divergence** | Semantic preservation without collapse | The comparator reports partial-set divergences (authored subset vs. observed superset, or vice versa) without conflating partial-match with full-divergence |
| **Multi-survivor mismatch** | Ambiguity handling under joint-survivor topology | The comparator reports divergence between authored single-survivor expectations and observed multi-survivor outcomes (and the inverse) without collapsing ambiguity into binary divergence |

The exercise produces:

  recomposition-arc test infrastructure) exercising each

### 2.3 Proactive scope guardrail

**Gate 4's divergence-shape robustness exercise occurs within
the chat-handler arbitration surface only.** No cross-surface
extension, no chain-step seeding, no broader-than-chat-handler
fixture identity, no comparator semantics extension.

Explicit rejections of broader scope language:

- **Not** *"cross-surface divergence."* That semantic class
  remains explicitly unbound per Gate 3 framing §6.3 +
  carrier #15's third clause. Gate 4 frames divergence
  shapes *within* the surface, not divergence *between*
  surfaces.
- **Not** *"semantic divergence."* The vocabulary "semantic"
  smuggles ontology the project has not earned at gate 4.
  Gate 4 frames divergence as *structural-shape* divergence
  (ordering / partial-set / multi-survivor cardinality)
  consumed by the comparator's structured `DivergenceReport`.
- **Not** *"divergence taxonomy."* A taxonomy implies a
  finished classification space. Gate 4 enumerates three
  dimensions because three architectural pressure layers
  have been operationally identified; it does not commit to
  the three being exhaustive. The framing explicitly leaves
  room for Gate 5 to surface additional dimensions if
  operational pressure surfaces them (§5.5's three-slot
  lock applies to *Gate 4 scope*, not to the universe of
  divergence shapes).

This scope guardrail is load-bearing wording. PR-level
documentation, fixture module docstrings, test module
docstrings, commit message bodies, and the §6 placement
language carry the guardrail verbatim. The wording prevents
ontology creep into either the comparator's vocabulary or
the methodology-stack claim language — particularly at the
moments most likely to fold a broader scope (drafting
Placement B's epistemic claim language; naming the §5.3
candidate methodology's third-instance corroboration target).

### 2.4 The architectural commitment

**Gate 4 is the deliberate continuation of empirically
bounded topology proof through divergence-shape robustness
exercise.**

This sentence is the §2.4 binding form. It travels verbatim
at:

- Each Gate 4 PR's framing/spec/close artifact bodies.
- Each Gate 4 PR commit message body under "architectural
  commitment" sections.
- Gate 4 close artifact §1 (gate-arc synthesis).
- Other major synthesis artifacts at gate scope (if any
  surface).

**The travel discipline deliberately stops short of fixture
+ test module docstrings.** The §2.4 sentence is **gate-
shaped architectural posture**, NOT carrier-shaped
governance and NOT candidate-carrier-corroboration substrate.
Heavy verbatim travel through subordinate surfaces would
risk category confusion: fixture and test docstrings carry
local operational constraints / topology-shape rationale /
carrier-bound invariants (which is why active carriers DO
travel through them per §6.1 — carriers are carrier-bound
invariants by definition). Forcing the §2.4 sentence through
those subordinate surfaces would treat the sentence as
proto-carrier content despite Gate 4 framing explicitly
rejecting that category (§3.1 + §6.1). The travel
discipline preserves the carrier / governing sentence /
methodology-stack distinction the §4 + §6 structure
operationalizes — the same category integrity Placement B's
introduction depends on.

The wording is structurally chosen against three drifts:

1. **Deferral-as-apology drift.** Path B at Gate 4 is *not*
   "another defer." The sentence reframes Path B as positive
   architectural action: empirical bounding is the constraint
   under which topology proof can occur, not a constraint
   that prevents it.

2. **Topology-invention drift.** "Bounded topology proof"
   excludes designing-without-grounding by definition. If a
   Gate 4 PR surfaces work that would constitute topology
   invention (cross-surface semantics, speculative chain-step
   topology, etc.), the work is rejected at the spec layer
   against the §2.4 commitment, not against an ad hoc
   per-PR guardrail.

3. **Scope-elasticity drift.** "Through divergence-shape
   robustness exercise" names the specific substrate axis
   Gate 4 commits to. Other substrate axes (e.g., new
   authority surfaces, persistence-topology robustness,
   walker mechanics consolidation) are out of scope at Gate 4
   regardless of how plausible they appear during
   implementation. Their introduction requires Gate 5
   framing-level decision.

§5.1 carries the binding-decision form of §2.4; §4.3 carries
the architectural delta framing; §6.3 carries the
methodology-stack interpretation Placement B encodes.

#### Dimension 1: Ordering divergence (purest topology pressure)

### 4.5 Causality discipline — three governance preconditions for Placement B

The Placement B claim is subject to three governance
preconditions. These are **part of the claim itself**, not
external clarifications. Absence of any one precondition
disqualifies the absence-evidence from contributing to
Placement B corroboration.

**Precondition 1: Prior pressure prediction at framing time.**

The framing artifact (Gate 4 framing OR the relevant PR's
framing/spec) must explicitly predict the cleanup-pressure
form that the absence outcome would otherwise constitute
evidence for. Retrospective "we always meant to prevent this"
narratives do NOT qualify; the prediction must precede
implementation.

Operational form at Gate 4: each Gate 4 PR's framing/spec
enumerates predicted cleanup-pressure forms (helper merger /
canonicalization pressure / partial-match collapse pressure
/ multi-survivor cardinality smoothing / etc.) BEFORE
implementation begins. The PR 10 + PR 11 framing precedents
established the pattern.

**Precondition 2: Named suppression mechanism.**

The framing must identify (a) the topology shape that would
generate the predicted pressure and (b) the constraint that
prevents the topology shape from materializing. The
suppression mechanism is named — wording that travels
verbatim into PR / test / fixture docstrings + commit message
bodies.

Operational form at Gate 4: each predicted pressure is paired
with a named suppression mechanism (e.g., "ordering-divergence
fixture tests the PR 10 §4.2 binding behavioral commitment;
the discipline prevents canonicalization pressure from
generating"). The naming creates traceability between
framing-time prediction and observed absence.

**Precondition 3: Corroborated recurrence across independent
conditions.**

A single instance of predicted-pressure absence is raw
archaeology, not evidence. The claim requires multi-instance
corroboration under genuinely independent conditions. The
existing two-instance §5.3 candidate methodology meets the
recurrence bar with PR 10 + PR 11 instances under independent
conditions (PR 10 was new-production-surface authoring; PR 11
was end-to-end recomposition exercise).

Operational form at Gate 4: the three primary PRs (PR 13 +
PR 14 + PR 15) each predict cleanup-pressure forms and
operationally test the suppression mechanisms. If pressure
forms remain absent across all three PRs under the three
distinct divergence dimensions, the three-instance
corroboration bar is independently met at Gate 4 (alongside
the §5.3 candidate's third-instance evidence). If pressure
forms surface at any PR, the methodology-stack claim is NOT
falsified — it is simply not corroborated at that PR; the
"resistance to surfaced pressure" evidence class registers
instead.

**The three preconditions are jointly binding.** Absence
evidence that meets any two but not the third does NOT
contribute to Placement B corroboration. The discipline
prevents the failure mode where "nothing went wrong" becomes
intrinsically meaningful without prior prediction + named
mechanism + corroborated recurrence.

Once a methodology starts legitimizing absence as evidence,
the evidentiary threshold must stay extremely disciplined or
the architecture becomes unfalsifiable. The three
preconditions are the guard against that drift.

| **A** | Operational | Corroboration substrate for a specific named methodology (§5.3 candidate) | Cleanup-pressure absence under framing-shaped suppression (single-methodology scope) | Third-instance ABSENCE-evidence under independent conditions |
| **B** | Epistemic | Classification claim about reliability evidence itself (methodology-stack architecture) | Peer-class elevation of causal-absence alongside resistance-to-surfaced-pressure | Three causality preconditions operationally manifest across independent divergence dimensions |

The two placements are **not redundant**. Placement A
corroborates a specific named methodology that contributes

evidence; Placement A is the operational substrate for the

the broader peer-class claim.

### 6.5 Causal absence vs. passive absence — the governance discipline

The three causality preconditions (§4.5) operationally
distinguish **causal absence** from **passive absence**:

| Form | Definition | Evidence status |
|---|---|---|
| **Causal absence** | Predicted pressure that did NOT surface because framing-shaped constraint successfully prevented the topology shape that would have generated it. Three preconditions met. | Reliability evidence per Placement B claim |
| **Passive absence** | Pressure that did NOT surface for reasons not traceable to framing-shaped constraint (e.g., the implementation happened not to encounter the surface, the test coverage happened not to exercise the form, the pressure was never predicted). Any one of the three preconditions missing. | Raw archaeology, NOT reliability evidence |

The distinction is **load-bearing**. Without it, Placement B
collapses into self-congratulating interpretation and becomes
unfalsifiable.

The governance discipline operates at three surfaces:

1. **Framing-time prediction** must precede implementation.
   Retrospective construction of predictions from observed
   outcomes is rejected — the prediction must be in the
   framing artifact (or PR framing/spec) before
   implementation begins.

2. **Named suppression mechanism** must accompany each
   prediction. "We predicted X would surface; X did not
   surface" without an explicit naming of *why* the framing-
   shaped constraint prevented X is reduced-rigor archaeology,
   not causal-evidence.

3. **Corroborated recurrence** across independent conditions
   must accumulate before naming promotion. Single-instance
   absence-evidence is raw archaeology; multi-instance
   absence-evidence under genuinely independent conditions
   meets the corroboration bar.

**The discipline is structural, not stylistic.** Future
Gate-X PRs proposing to register absence-evidence without
all three preconditions met are rejected at the spec layer
against Placement B's binding form + the three preconditions.
