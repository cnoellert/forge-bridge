# v1.9 Conversational Reads — Restoring the Answer-Pass as a Pressure Instrument

> **What this artifact is.** Cycle-1 writing-room framing for v1.9,
> drafted from the ratified three-voice seed
> `SEED-CHAT-SYNTHESIS-CALIBRATION-V1.9.md`. It follows the compact
> convention ([[feedback-cadence-artifacts-shrink-to-load-bearing]]) —
> rulings + grounding + forward-pointers — and carries the v1.8 §Decisions
> consequence-analysis layer, because v1.9 dissolves a constraint the last
> several milestones treated as law and the room needs the consequence map
> to vote on the replacement.
>
> **What this artifact is not.** Not a roadmap, not a phase plan, not a
> reopening of substrate authority. The ratification / preview / assent
> discipline v1.6–v1.8 built is sound and stays closed. v1.9 touches the
> **read** answer surface only.
>
> **The one-line thesis.** The chat stopped answering humans (A.1), and
> every phase since drifted further by tightening a verbatim-projection
> discipline that *structurally forbids the synthesis a human answer is*.
> v1.9 restores a model answer-pass on **reads** — not as a polished
> product, but as the **pressure instrument** that lets a human finally
> use the tool and surface the failures worth fixing.

## The transition

v1.6 made the substrate operable. v1.7 shipped the artist surfaces
(`discover`, asset tools, chat compile → preview → ratify → apply). v1.8
completed the Console authority projection — and in closing CA.1, named
the thing every prior milestone missed: **the chat is a command console,
not chat.** It returns dispatch envelopes, not answers.

Grounded against the codebase 2026-05-31:

- `chat_handler` (`handlers.py:1300`) routes a compiled intent through
  `run_compile_branch`. The non-mutating **read** result is emitted as a
  `chain_complete` envelope (`handlers.py:1161`): `{chain: [...],
  stop_reason: "chain_complete", preview: None}` — dispatch results, **no
  `messages`, no model-authored `final_text`.**
- The mutating result is `preview_emitted` (`handlers.py:1140`) carrying
  the structured `preview` + `graph_intent_id`.
- `final_text` is `""` by deliberate Phase A decision (`handlers.py:
  784-786`); the only regime that still returns a human sentence
  (`messages`) is the legacy `tool_forced` loop (`:787`) — the *old*
  agentic talking path A.1 replaced.

**So the read path has a clean seam with no author at the end of it.**
A `chain_complete` carries the truth (the chain results) and stops. v1.9
reattaches an author at exactly that seam — for reads only.

## Provenance — there is no no-synthesis law to reverse

The seed's git dig (carried verbatim as binding context, not re-litigated
here): two unrelated decisions were fused into a principle never actually
adopted.

1. **A.1 dropped the model answer-pass for COST** — determinism +
   token/latency budget, not principle. It made reads emit envelopes.
2. **24.4 coined the ORCHESTRATOR-impersonation guard** (`router.py:
   639-662`, commit `34bc31a`): "orchestrator may terminate but does not
   impersonate the model." Narrow and correct — the *deterministic control
   layer* gained authority to terminate a loop (K=2) and must not then
   fabricate model output. 24.5 extended it to consumer projection.

The compression "control flow not meaning" cited as ancient doctrine
**first appears in `bb109e6` — the CA.1 close written last session.** It
was never established law. There is therefore nothing to "reverse": a real
guard to KEEP (orchestrator non-impersonation) and a performance decision
to UN-MAKE (restore the model answer-pass on reads).

## Recalibrated doctrine

The substrate-authority laws v1.6–v1.8 ratified are **not reopened.** What
recalibrates is the scope of "no synthesis":

- **The cut line.** The system MAY synthesize **explanations of facts that
  exist** in the substrate. It MAY NOT synthesize **facts that do not
  exist.** Invent nothing — not state, not results, not authority
  decisions, not provenance, not predicted outcomes as fact.
- **The objective is understanding, not projection.**
  `Substrate Truth → Grounded Interpretation → Human Understanding` — not
  `Substrate Truth → Projection`. An operator must never have to perform
  the final interpretation step by hand because the system *can* explain
  its own evidence but *chooses not to*. (That choosing-not-to is exactly
  what CA.1 shipped.)
- **The two-axis gate** (the human-legibility gate CA.1 lacked). Every
  read response is judged on TWO independent questions: **(1) grounded?**
  (every claim traces to substrate) AND **(2) understandable?** (a
  non-developer artist reads it as an answer). Grounded-not-understandable
  = incomplete (today's console). Understandable-not-grounded = unsafe.
  v1.9 requires both, as a framing-time acceptance criterion.
- **The authorship axis** (the operative cut). Same grounded sentence,
  different emitter, different verdict. Three legitimate authors:
  *consumer rendering* (deterministic template — free, under-used),
  *substrate enrichment* (`{reason_code, human_reason}` — no author
  question), *model synthesis attributed* (taxon `done` — the priced one).
  The surviving guard, unchanged: the **orchestrator/handler may NOT
  author prose**, and **assent stays the operator's** (narration ≠ assent).

## The spine — natural language as the pressure instrument

The seed left a genuine open question: *is the model read-path worth its
reliability cost, or does enrichment+rendering get far enough on reads?*

**The room resolved the sequencing this session, and it inverts the
seed's cautious default.** The cheap-tier-first ordering (ship enrichment
+ rendering, hold the model read-path) has a hole: it polishes the
*rendering of failures* on a surface that produces no real usage. The
failures worth rendering only exist once a human can drive the tool in
natural language. Render failure-reasons first and you render *synthetic*
failures — the ones we imagined, not the ones an artist hits. That is the
CA.1 UAT theater one layer up.

**Therefore the spine is: stand up a rough model answer-pass on reads as a
pressure instrument — not a shipped product.** This is the
[[project-substrate-pressure-two-stroke]] engine: the pressure stroke is
currently *blocked because the tool is illegible*, and the instrument that
unblocks it is natural-language reads. It is also measure-first, this
project's grain — the seed's "what convergence evidence would close it"
becomes evidence generated *by use*, not by planning. The frustration of
using a rough instrument is not a blocker; **it is the data.**

The enrichment / consumer-rendering tiers do not vanish — they get
**ranked by the real failure corpus** the instrument produces, instead of
guessed at in advance.

### The guards that survive even in spike posture

Straight from the seed, binding on the spike:

- **Reads only.** Restore the answer-pass on the read path — the seed puts
  it there precisely because there is **no authority record to corrupt.**
- **Mutations untouched.** Deterministic preview + ratify, no model prose.
  The spike does not go near the assent path or `preview_emitted`.
- **Orchestrator stays mute.** The model authors the sentence, attributed
  (`done`); never the handler. Keep 24.4 (`router.py:639-662`).
- **Fidelity clause** (Part 6). On the model path, grounding is necessary
  but not sufficient: the sentence must also not overstate
  certainty / tense / causality (a *preview* described as if it
  *happened*). Reads have no `would/did` modal hazard the way mutations
  do, but multi-result reasoning can still imply false causation —
  resolve the clause's enforcement shape at framing-close.

## Scope

v1.9 is **one pressure-stroke milestone**, deliberately small:

- **The spike** — a model answer-pass on Console + chat read queries,
  reattached at the `chain_complete` seam (`handlers.py:1161`), emitting an
  attributed model sentence as the answer. Rough is acceptable; the bar is
  *usable enough to dogfood*, not *production-converged*.
- **The dogfood** — real artist-first usage under the
  [[project-forge-bridge-ux-philosophy]] (non-developer UAT). This is not a
  closing formality; it is the milestone's actual product — the failure
  corpus that ranks everything after it.
- **The legibility tier rides behind the corpus, not ahead of it.**
  Consumer rendering + substrate enrichment of failure/outcome envelopes
  land *prioritized by what the dogfood surfaced*, not pre-built. (Whether
  any of this tier lands inside v1.9 or seeds v1.10 is Q-4.)

## Out of scope

- **SEED-AUTH-V1.5** — auth identity binding stays carry-forward to its own
  dedicated milestone; `AssentRecord.decided_by` stays free-string. v1.9
  touches reads, which author no authority record, so auth does not bind
  the spine.
- **The mutation / assent path.** `preview_emitted`, ratify, apply,
  `AssentRecord` — all unchanged. No model prose anywhere near assent.
- **Orchestrator-authored prose.** Keep 24.4. The control layer terminates;
  it does not speak.
- **New graph primitives / entity types / substrate authority.** v1.9 adds
  an answer-pass over existing substrate; it does not extend the substrate.
- **Production convergence-closure of the read-path.** The spike's job is
  to *generate* the convergence evidence (latency, tool-selection, recurrence
  under the qwen tool prefix), not to *close* it. Closing it is downstream,
  scoped by what the corpus shows.
- **Calling cheap enrichment "chat."** The seed's standing warning (Part 7):
  legible ≠ answering. The model read-path is the part that makes it chat;
  the green gate on easy queries must not hide that the hard tier shipped.

## Decisions & open questions

> Leans given where the room's lead-with-views convention expects one.
> Q-1 is the genuine fork; the rest carry to the spike's discuss-phase.

- **Q-1: answer-pass mechanism. OPEN → the real fork.** Two shapes:
  - **(a) full agentic answer-pass on reads** — model calls read tool →
    reads result → writes a sentence; can reason across multiple results
    ("what changed on this sequence since yesterday, does it affect the
    hero shot?"). Re-inherits the 24.x convergence wall head-on.
  - **(b) terminal synthesis pass** — keep compile-then-dispatch, add a
    single model "explain this `chain_complete` to a human" turn at the
    seam. Cheaper, preserves A.1's determinism, but cannot reason across
    results it didn't pre-compile.

  *Lean: (a), in spike posture.* The instrument's job is to surface the
  convergence failures the room keeps deferring; (b) sidesteps exactly the
  failure mode we need to observe. Decision-impact: (a) makes the spike's
  reliability rougher up front but its failure corpus *complete*; (b)
  produces a smoother instrument that under-samples the hard case. Genuine
  fork — resolve at the spike's discuss-phase against a live latency probe.

- **Q-2: phase decomposition. OPEN → punt to discuss.** Likely a single
  spike phase (instrument + dogfood) with the legibility tier seeded for a
  follow-on ranked by the corpus. *Lean: do not pre-decompose the
  legibility work — let the failure data shape it, per the spine.*

- **Q-3: fidelity-clause enforcement. OPEN → framing-close or discuss.**
  How is "no overstatement of certainty/tense/causality" on the model path
  policed — prompt-level instruction, a structured grounding check, or
  dogfood-observed and corrected? *Lean: dogfood-observed first; the
  instrument exists to find where it lies, not to be pre-hardened against
  every lie.*

- **Q-4: does the legibility tier land inside v1.9?** Or does v1.9 close on
  the spike + corpus and seed the ranked enrichment work for v1.10? *Lean:
  v1.9 closes on the instrument + the corpus; enrichment is the next
  stroke, ranked by evidence — keeping the milestone a clean single
  pressure stroke per the two-stroke rule.*

- **Q-5: the convergence-evidence question itself.** The seed names this the
  v1.9 spine "turned into an evidence question." What measurements does the
  dogfood need to capture to *close* (not just observe) the read-path
  reliability question — first-token latency, end-to-end wall-clock under
  the 58-tool prefix, tool-selection accuracy on iter 1, recurrence shape?
  *This is the spike's instrumentation spec; surface it at discuss.*
