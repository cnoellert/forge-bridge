## Theme — translation success became a *separately measurable verdict*

C2's live E2E proof (v1.12) relocated the frontier: the substrate is trustworthy and execution is solved, so the weak point is the path **from human language into the substrate**. v1.13 formalized, bounded, and *validated* the translation machinery that already ran scattered and implicit (`compile_intent`, tool filter/narrow, `resolve_query_entities`, `extract_explicit_params`, routing) — **not** a greenfield build, **not** "make chat smarter." The translation/substrate boundary is the **chain-step graph**; translation and substrate are now scored independently (`[[feedback-substrate-pass-translation-open]]`).

## The two durable findings

1. **Fixability is governed by faithful CONTENT-recoverability — orthogonal to the well-formedness/content tier** (TF.4). The 2×2: `detached_args`/`trailing_empty` (malformed + recoverable → fixed); `non_tool_step` (malformed + non-recoverable → detector-only); `space-mangle` (well-formed + non-recoverable → detector+prevention). The off-diagonal cells prove the real axis is *"is the faithful content present?"*, not *"is it structural?"* Builds on `[[feedback-wellformedness-precedes-content]]`.
2. **The system got better by deleting a dependency, not repairing data** (TF.3b). A bad label was retired as a *verdict source* (`bad label → stop reading the label`), not repaired (`bad label → fix label`). The bidirectional 7/15 mismatch forced it — a field moving both above and below reality is the wrong abstraction, not stale data. **TF.3b does not repair `expected_well_formed`; it retires it as a verdict source.**

## Honest scope (no overclaim)
v1.13 made translation fidelity **measurable and the recoverable defects fixed** — it did **not** make every translation correct. The dominant content failure (entity value-fidelity / space-mangle) has a detector and prevention but **no deterministic guarantee** (blocked on external ground-truth → desktop-wiring, gated). The oracle measures; the deterministic repairs covered exactly the content-recoverable cells. That is the milestone's true boundary, not a gap.

## The two-stroke read — next pass is human-pressure, not substrate

The substrate is now **measured, instrumented, and corrected** enough. Per `[[project-substrate-pressure-two-stroke]]`, accumulating another substrate milestone would drift the tool back toward a console. The next pressure point is **human-facing workflow**: how the system communicates uncertainty, failure, preview, correction, and intent back to the operator. v1.14 should be a human-pressure milestone (the natural successor to v1.9 Conversational Reads, now standing on a fidelity substrate that can tell a real translation failure from a substrate gap).

## Lessons banked
- **Read-before-scoping** caught misclassifications at every TF.4 slice and grounded the TF.3b single-site sweep.
- **Measure-first can say "don't build"** — it killed two prediction-driven slices and gated the deterministic guarantee.
- **Getting better by deleting a dependency** (TF.3b) — a new correction pattern, candidate for promotion.
- **The instrument must precede the optimization** — TF.3a/3b split honored `[[feedback-audit-before-overfit]]`; you cannot measure-first with an unvalidated oracle.
