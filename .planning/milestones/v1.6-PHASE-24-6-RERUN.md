## §5 Wall-clock honest analysis

**Operator-perceived total wait per warm probe:**

| Rerun | Warm wall-clock | Iter count | Per-iter avg |
|---|---|---|---|
| 24.4 | 54.7s | 4 | 13.7s |
| 24.5 | 51.3s | 4 | 12.8s |
| 24.6 (warm-2..5 avg) | 59.1s | 2 | 29.6s |

24.6 is **not dramatically faster** in raw wall-clock terms. Per-iter cost actually rises (~30s vs ~13s) because:

- 24.4/24.5 had 4 iters where 2 were cheap `flame_find_media` `tool_error` returns (~3-5s each — fast OS-level rejection on a query-string-required input) and 2 were expensive `flame_execute_python` canonical dispatches (~25-30s each).
- 24.6 has 2 iters, both expensive `flame_execute_python` canonical dispatches (~30s each) — no cheap fail-iters to bring the per-iter average down.

Net result: **operator wall-clock per warm probe is roughly comparable** between 24.6 and 24.5 (~60s vs ~51s in steady state). 24.6 trades 4 cheap+expensive iters for 2 expensive iters. The work done is the same; the model's first guess is now correct.

**Where the operator-visible win actually lives:**

- **Determinism.** K=2 fires at iter 2 every time, not at iter 4. The termination shape is predictable, which matters for operator mental model and for cold-budget headroom.
- **Affordance correctness.** The model picks the right tool first, which matters for any future scenario where iter-1 selection has higher cost than a tool-error rejection (e.g., a future Anthropic-routed path where iter-1 mis-selection costs more than ~5s).
- **Visible coherence.** The CLI shows "iterations: 2" with both canonical results matching — operators can see at a glance that the model behaved deterministically rather than recovering from a mis-select.

**Where the operator-visible win does NOT live:**

- Raw "fewer seconds per chat call." 24.6 is not the wall-clock win it might naively appear to be.
- Cold-start fit. The cold probe still budget-exhausts at 130s — same as 24.4 and 24.5. Description sharpening cannot mechanically shrink cold-start LLM inference cost.

This honest framing matters for §11 carry-forward decisions: 24.6 closes the affordance pathology; it does NOT close cold-start budget gaps or per-iter inference cost. Those remain separate concerns with their own re-eval triggers in the §11 register.

## §6 What this rerun does NOT prove

Per FRAMING §1 "What the evidence does and does not prove" + §5 multi-dimensional pass criterion:

- **Does not prove the same shift holds for other query classes.** The forcing function tested one canonical query ("What are the clips on Reel 1"). Other query shapes the model might mis-select on (e.g., "list the segments on the current sequence", "show me the batch graph") were not probed. Generalizing 24.6's win to those query classes is intentionally unbound — that would be 24.7's question.
- **Does not prove the catalogue sweep is unnecessary.** §3 explicitly deferred the full `flame_*` catalogue sweep. 24.6's narrow two-tool edit is sufficient for this query class; whether other latent affordance ambiguities exist in the 12 unmodified `flame_*` tools is a separate empirical question.
- **Does not prove M=5 is statistically definitive.** 5 reruns is a small sample. The 5/5 win is striking but a future operator could observe a 4/5 or 3/5 mix under different cache states, prompt-context shifts, or model-side stochasticity. The forcing function intentionally set the bar at ≥4/5 to leave room for one-shot variance; the 5/5 observed result exceeds that bar but does not eliminate the possibility of future selection drift.
- **Does not prove cold-prefix budget improves.** Cold probe still budget-exhausts at 130s. Description sharpening is orthogonal to cold-start inference cost.

## §10 Methodology notes

### §10.1 Daemon restart was load-bearing

The MCP daemon caches tool schemas at registration time (server startup). The first rerun attempt (`bbzoh0ny5`) aborted at pre-flight because the daemon held OLD docstrings — pre-flight check (originally `fbridge actions show <name>` grep) initially used incorrect CLI syntax; once corrected to direct Python module introspection (`from forge_bridge.tools.utility import execute_python; assert "canonical introspection surface" in execute_python.__doc__`), the pre-flight passed and the rerun executed. Pre-flight discipline saved a noisy rerun cycle that would have produced 24.5-shaped data.

### §10.2 The warm-1 partial-cold-warm signature

warm-1 elapsed 118s vs warm-2..5's ~60s. The preceding cold probe (130s budget exhaust) did NOT successfully warm the model's KV cache — the cold probe's iter-1 inference completed but loop budget ran out before iter-2 could start, leaving the cache in a partial-warm state. warm-1 paid the remaining warm-up cost on its iter-1 inference, then warm-2..5 hit the steady-state cache. This is a real signature of Phase 24.1 KV-cache dynamics; not a 24.6 anomaly.

### §10.3 Vacuous satisfaction is a stronger outcome than the framing anticipated

FRAMING §5 expected a measurement scenario where iter-1 selection might shift partially (e.g., 3/5) with iter-3 stabilization as the secondary win. The observed 5/5 makes iter-3 stabilization a vacuous question. Per [[feedback-distinct-success-criteria-per-adjacent-layer]], it's worth naming that vacuous satisfaction at the prior-stabilization dimension reflects a stronger-than-expected initial-prior shift, not an absence of evidence at that dimension. The §5 framing was structured to handle the partial case; the actual outcome simply doesn't require it.

### §10.4 The 2 broken tests as an in-scope D-04 consequence

Phase 23.1 pinned exact docstring strings in `test_flame_execute_python.py` to prevent regression. 24.6's intentional perturbation of those exact strings caused the pins to fail. Per FRAMING §13 footnote, this is a D-04 fix-as-discovered consequence of the in-scope edit, not a scope expansion. Commit 2.5 (`0011f1d`) updates the pins to match 24.6's perturbed language; the SPIRIT of the 23.1 pins (canonical-surface framing + escalation rule) is preserved at the new shape.

### §10.5 Cold path improvement is incidental, not load-bearing

Cold probe iter-1 picked `flame_execute_python` (24.4/24.5 cold picked `flame_find_media`). This is a small win — cold-path affordance is also corrected by the same docstring edit — but it does NOT make the cold probe fit in budget. Per §5 cold probes are anti-scope verification only; this improvement is recorded for archaeology but does not factor into pass-criterion evaluation.
