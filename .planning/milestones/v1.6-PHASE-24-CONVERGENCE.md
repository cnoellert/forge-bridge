# v1.6 Phase 24 — Writer's-Room Convergence Artifact

> **What this is.** A narrow writer's-room convergence on 5 of the 20 open questions in `v1.6-FRAMING.md` §12 — specifically the questions that materially affect emission shape, replay semantics, graph storage granularity, and chat/tool provenance for Phase 24. It is the substrate-decision-minimum that unblocks Phase 24 implementation work without prematurely hardening v1.6 as a whole.
>

## §2 — Phase 24's brutal scope

Phase 24 (Operability minimum) proves exactly four things. Nothing else.

### §2.1 — `flame_execute_python` is discoverable

A non-author operator deploying bridge v1.6 against a fresh conda env can, through normal use of the chat surface or the MCP tool list, reach `flame_execute_python` without author tribal knowledge about its existence or canonical-introspection-surface role. The Phase 23.1 docstring repositioning is the substrate; Phase 24 validates discoverability under non-author operator load.

### §2.2 — Chat converges reliably on Flame introspection

The canonical regression query `"What are the clips on Reel 1"` (§4) converges end-to-end on the production routing path within the 125s outer cap, with the chat trace showing `flame_execute_python` was reached as the substrate's intended escalation path. Phase 23.1 verified this once on author-walk; Phase 24 makes it operationally reliable — meaning a non-author operator can reproduce it, and a CI smoke can be wired to detect regression.

### §2.3 — Proto-node emission works across at least one surface cleanly

`flame_execute_python` invocations emit proto-node records (shape per §5.4) to a JSONL stream as defined in §5.3. "Cleanly" means: the records parse, the shape conforms to the converged minimum, and the JSONL stream is readable by a downstream consumer (Phase 24 ships one — likely a `fbridge graph debug` read-only command or equivalent; sufficient evidence that the records are structurally useful per §2.4).

"Across at least one surface" means Phase 24 commits only to proto-node emission from `flame_execute_python`. Other surfaces (Console exec view, learning pipeline JSONL, staged-ops table) are *not* required to emit proto-node records in Phase 24. If they do, that's a Phase 24+1 convergence; if they don't, v1.6 still works because the substrate is one-surface-validated.

### §2.4 — The resulting records are structurally useful

Phase 24's success criterion includes evidence that the emitted proto-node records can be consumed for at least one operationally useful purpose — minimally, replaying a chat session's tool-invocation arc, or surfacing tool-discovery patterns. The criterion is *evidence of usefulness*, not *full graph-runtime activation*. If the records exist and a 50-line consumer script demonstrates one operational use, §2.4 closes.

### §2.5 — That's it

Phase 24 does not ship four-plus features. It ships *four proofs against one minimal substrate*. The scope is intentionally narrow so the substrate decisions in §5 don't get prematurely-hardened by feature-ambition pulling additional surfaces into Phase 24.

## §3 — Phase 24's brutal anti-scope

The following are explicitly NOT in Phase 24, and are NOT failure of Phase 24 if absent at Phase 24 close. They are deferred for architectural reasons, not scheduling reasons.

### §3.1 — Graph visualization

`SEED-NODE-SCHEMATIC-V1.6+` and `v1.6-FRAMING.md` §1.1 articulate the schematic-as-visual-graph product surface. Phase 24 emits proto-nodes; it does NOT render them. Visualization belongs to a later phase because (a) rendering requires the substrate to exist first, and (b) trying to ship both forces the substrate decisions in §5 to absorb rendering-shape constraints they shouldn't carry yet.

### §3.2 — Graph execution

v1.6-FRAMING.md §9 articulates the three-tier runtime loop (deterministic / LLM / staged-ops gating). Phase 24 does not execute graphs — it emits records that describe operations as they happen against the existing linear executor. Graph-shaped execution is v1.7+ per `v1.6-FRAMING.md` framing. Conflating "emit graph-shaped records" with "execute the graph" would invert the framing's transitional-not-graph-native commitment.

### §3.3 — Subgraph mining / promotion

v1.6-FRAMING.md §6 articulates subgraph promotion as structural collapse. Phase 24 does not mine or promote. The substrate must exist with one-surface-validated proto-node emission before mining-shape decisions are even tractable; preempting that with Phase 24 mining work would force §5.4 (record shape) to absorb mining-shape constraints prematurely.

### §3.4 — Registry migration

The existing tool registry + manifest substrate continues unchanged in Phase 24. Migration to a graph-shape registry is v1.6.x+ scope. Phase 24's proto-node records describe *what tool was invoked*; they do not replace the tool registry's authoritative record of *what tools exist*.

### §3.5 — Why these stay out

The framing in `v1.6-FRAMING.md` separated **runtime truth** from **product surface** for exactly this reason. Phase 24 ships runtime-truth substrate. The product surfaces (visualization, execution, mining, registry migration) build on that substrate in later phases. Pulling any of them into Phase 24 erodes that separation and produces an architectural commitment v1.6 should not yet make.

### §4.1 — Reframing the residual

Three v1.5 carry-forward seeds (`SEED-PHASE-20-NON-AUTHOR-UAT-V1.6+` / `SEED-PHASE-20.1-ARTIST-UAT-V1.6+` / `SEED-PHASE-21-NON-AUTHOR-UAT-V1.6+`) carry the non-author UAT residual into v1.6. The non-author UAT *is* how Phase 24 closes. Without a non-author operator successfully exercising §2.1–§2.4 against the shipped substrate, Phase 24 has not proved what it set out to prove.

### §4.2 — The question Phase 24 is actually asking

The original v1.5 forcing function asked: *can the bridge be installed?* Phase 20.1 walk-validation answered that under author-walk + 4 reference operator machines.

Phase 24 asks something more sophisticated: *can someone who didn't build the semantic map discover and successfully use the canonical introspection surface?*

That is a different test. The author knows `flame_execute_python` exists because the author registered it. The author knows the docstring positions it as the canonical introspection surface because the author wrote the docstring. The author knows the chat path escalates to it on the canonical regression query because the author verified that on portofino.

A non-author has none of those advantages. The substrate must teach them — through tool discoverability, through chat-path convergence, through emitted records that explain what happened — without the author acting as the semantic router.

### §4.3 — The canonical regression query as multi-layered test

The query `"What are the clips on Reel 1"` (verbatim, with that exact capitalization and spacing) operationally tests at minimum four orthogonal properties in one sentence:

1. **Semantic retrieval** — can the model interpret "Reel 1" as the Flame-substrate concept the operator means?
2. **Affordance discoverability** — does the model find `flame_execute_python` when the narrow `flame_*` tools don't fit?
3. **Tool-discoverability** — is the canonical-introspection docstring legible enough to bias the model toward escalation?
4. **Graph-runtime legitimacy** — does the resulting proto-node record stream describe what happened in a way a non-author can read back?

In Phase 23.1 the query tested (1) + (2) + (3). Phase 24 adds (4). The query becomes the operational bellwether for v1.6 substrate work — every Phase 24 close must demonstrate convergence on this query under the §2 four proofs, against non-author operator load.

### §4.4 — Formalization commitments

Phase 24 must formalize the canonical regression query as:

- **A shared fixture** at a known location (likely `tests/fixtures/canonical_introspection_query.py` or equivalent), with the verbatim query string + expected substrate-property assertions per §4.3.
- **A canonical operational regression** — every v1.6+ phase that touches the chat path, MCP registry, or proto-node emission MUST verify the fixture before close.
- **A CI smoke candidate** — when the testing infrastructure can carry a live-Flame-dependent smoke (separate seed; out of Phase 24's immediate scope but worth naming), the fixture migrates to that infrastructure as a gate.

This is what `SEED-CANONICAL-FLAME-INTROSPECTION-QUERY-V1.6+` already names; this convergence artifact treats that seed as load-bearing for Phase 24, not as deferred-decoration.

## §5 — The five blocker questions (converged positions)

These five questions block Phase 24 implementation because they shape the proto-node record substrate that §2.3 emits. The remaining 15 §12 questions do not block Phase 24 and stay intentionally unresolved (§6).

Positions below lead with the recommendation + reasoning. The writer's-room cadence trusts the correction cycle.

### §5.1 — Append-only vs in-place status mutation (§12.1.1 in v1.6-FRAMING.md)

**Position: append-only.** Every status change emits a new record (`created` → `started` → `completed`/`failed`). No in-place mutation of prior records.

**Reasoning:**
- Replay semantics are simpler under append-only — no "what was the prior state" ambiguity.
- The bridge already runs two patterns: JSONL execution log is append-only; `staged_operation` table is in-place. Proto-node emission inherits the append-only pattern, aligning with the JSONL substrate it builds on.
- Append-only is the more demanding constraint. Cheap to relax later if cost surfaces; hard to add if you start in-place.
- Matches v1.6-FRAMING.md's runtime-truth-as-event-stream framing (§9, three-tier loop).

**Cost accepted:** current state of a node is derived by projection (replay-fold), not stored. This is added structure on consumers, not on emitters.

**What stays unresolved:** retention policy for the append-only log (§12.2.7) — not a Phase 24 blocker because Phase 24 doesn't operate at retention scale.

### §5.2 — Node ID format (§12.1.4 in v1.6-FRAMING.md)

**Position: two-ID strategy.** ULID for instance IDs (per-event-record `node_id`); content-hash for type IDs (tool kinds, vocabulary kinds, anything that's intrinsically content-defined).

**Reasoning:**
- ULIDs are lexically sortable + time-ordered, which composes naturally with §5.1 append-only event stream (records sort by emission time when sorted by ID).
- ULIDs are already in the codebase elsewhere — no new external dependency.
- Content-hashing tool nodes is valuable for dedup at the registry surface (Phase 7 PROV-01..06 already established `code_hash` as canonical for synthesized tools). Reusing this for type-ID composes cleanly.
- The two-ID strategy maps onto v1.6-FRAMING.md's two-graph model (§3): instance graph carries ULIDs; type graph carries content-hashes.

**Resistance considered:** UUIDv7 is the framing's lean (§12.1.4 verbatim: "UUIDv7 is sortable + standard; the framing leans here"). Three reasons I argue against UUIDv7 here: (a) ULIDs are already deployed in the codebase, (b) UUIDv7 sortability buys nothing ULIDs don't, (c) UUIDv7 standardization helps with cross-tool interop that Phase 24 isn't doing. If v1.7+ surfaces a cross-system interop need, UUIDv7 migration is a one-format conversion — preserve the option as deferred.

**What stays unresolved:** schema versioning policy across IDs (§12.1.5) — not a Phase 24 blocker.

### §5.3 — JSONL granularity (§12.2.6 in v1.6-FRAMING.md)

**Position: one record per node-event.** Where node-event = creation, status transition (start/complete/fail), or other discrete substrate happenings (propose, approve, etc., when those surfaces come online later). Granularity matches §5.1 append-only stance.

**File granularity:** **one file per graph**, where "graph" for Phase 24's purposes is the chat session ID (a chat turn's tool-invocation arc is one graph). This matches v1.6-FRAMING.md §12.2.6 lean ("per-graph is cleanest for replay"). Phase 24 has no log-rotation problem at one-graph-per-file because chat sessions are bounded.

**Reasoning:**
- One-record-per-event is the minimum granularity that supports §5.1 replay semantics.
- One-file-per-graph keeps replay localized — no need to scan a global log to reconstruct a session.
- Path convention: `~/.forge-bridge/graphs/<chat_session_id>.jsonl` — matches existing JSONL path conventions for execution log.

**Cost accepted:** higher inode count, more file-handle pressure. Not a Phase 24-scale concern; revisit at retention-policy time (§12.2.7).

**What stays unresolved:** concurrency on multi-surface emission (§12.2.8) — not a Phase 24 blocker because Phase 24 emits from one surface (`flame_execute_python`).

### §5.4 — Minimal graph record shape

**Position: six required fields, payload-extensible.** Every proto-node record in Phase 24 carries:

```json
{
  "node_id": "01HXYZ...",                    // ULID per §5.2
  "node_kind": "flame_execute_python",       // closed enumeration per v1.6-FRAMING.md §4
  "event_kind": "created" | "started" | "completed" | "failed",
  "timestamp": "2026-05-14T12:34:56.789Z",   // ISO-8601 with millisecond precision
  "parent_id": null | "<ULID>",              // graph-parent reference; null for root events
  "payload": { ... }                          // kind-specific opaque blob (see Phase 24 conventions)
}
```

**Reasoning:**
- 6 fields is the minimum that supports §5.1 (append-only event reconstruction), §5.2 (ULID instance + parent_id reference), §5.3 (one-event-per-record), and §4 (replay-readable by non-author).
- Phase 24 does NOT pre-commit to payload shape. The payload field is the discovery surface — Phase 24 will surface what shape works through implementation; Phase 24+1 may converge it.
- Phase 24 does NOT include `provenance.user` (§12.1.2 unresolved), `metrics` (§12.1.3 unresolved), or `schema_version` (§12.1.5 unresolved). Pragmatic placeholders:
  - `provenance.user` lives in payload temporarily as `payload.user = os.getuser()` — clearly Phase-24-only, replaceable when §12.1.2 converges.
  - `metrics` lives in payload temporarily as `payload.metrics = {...}` — same disposition.
  - `schema_version` is implicit in Phase 24 as `"1.6.0-phase24"` (stamped at file-creation, NOT per-record). When §12.1.5 converges, the file-level stamp migrates to wherever §12.1.5 lands.

**What stays unresolved:** the payload shape itself. Phase 24 surfaces this through implementation; do not pre-commit at convergence.

### §5.5 — Response-shape strategy for `/api/v1/chat` (§12.3.9 in v1.6-FRAMING.md)

**Position: out-of-band JSONL emission, no chat response-shape change in Phase 24.** Proto-node records emit to the JSONL stream defined in §5.3. The `/api/v1/chat` response shape stays exactly as it is in v1.5.

**Reasoning:**
- A response-shape change is an API-contract change. CLI consumers, MCP consumers, and any external integrators that hit `/api/v1/chat` would need to adapt.
- Out-of-band emission is the minimum-viable proof of §2.3 (proto-node emission works). It does NOT pre-commit to a streaming-response architecture Phase 24 doesn't need.
- If Phase 25+ surfaces a real need to surface graph records through the chat response (e.g., Console renders nodes inline; Foundry needs them in the response for staging), the response-shape migration is a separately-bounded change — not Phase 24 scope.
- Matches §3.5 separation: runtime truth lives in JSONL; the chat API surface stays a product surface decision for later phases.

**Cost accepted:** the chat surface is "disconnected" from the graph surface in Phase 24. The Console execs view (or a debug consumer per §2.4) bridges them out-of-band. This is acceptable because Phase 24's job is to prove emission works, not to surface it through chat.

**What stays unresolved:** Console execs view rendering (§12.6.19) and graph-store doctor check (§12.6.18) — neither is a Phase 24 blocker, both are natural Phase 25 convergence candidates.
