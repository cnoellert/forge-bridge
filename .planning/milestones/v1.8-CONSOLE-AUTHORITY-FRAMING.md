## Doctrine — authority-surface parity (ratified)

**Authority over the same substrate primitive must not be arbitrarily
restricted to a single operator surface.** Ratified by the room at v1.8
framing (Q-3). Narrowly scoped: this governs *authority-class operators*
— substrate mutations the operator authors (ratify, approve, reject) —
not parity of all features. It does not say every surface exposes
everything. It says an operator's authority over a substrate primitive
should not depend on which surface they happen to live on.

**Motivating instance.** Ratification: A.2 shipped it CLI-only; the
Console projection is missing. A.2 resulted in a substrate-first split
that left UI projection for a later milestone — the grounding shows that
outcome, not a documented phased-rollout intent.

**Architectural derivation.** The doctrine is structurally implied by
the substrate, not inferred from pattern recurrence. The dispatch
substrate is consumer-agnostic: `_execute_python_core`, `_engine.py`,
and `_step.py` do not know CLI from Console. When the authority path
runs through substrate that cannot distinguish its caller, restricting
authority to one caller surface is a surface accident, not an
architectural property.

**Why single-instance ratification is legitimate here (F-3).**
Architectural doctrines in forge-bridge ratify from structural
derivation, not from multi-instance recurrence — that bar belongs to
methodology-memory promotion, where recurrence *is* the evidence.
Conflating the two is a category error. The governing precedent is
architectural: "LLM never owns assent" (ratified A.2-FRAMING on one
motivating instance) and "substrate self-views are first-class operator
surfaces" (ratified `v1.7-ARTIST-READINESS-FRAMING.md:56` on first
articulation). Both held single-instance because they were derivable
from substrate shape. Authority-surface parity belongs to that family.

## The two surfaces

The authority chain has two consumer surfaces, both ratifying the same
substrate event:

- **CLI** — `fbridge ratify <graph_intent_id>` (shipped A.2). Calls
  `POST /api/v1/ratify`. Power-user surface. Scriptable.
- **Console** — chat preview projection + ratify affordance +
  ratification ledger view (this milestone). Artist-facing surface.
  Visual. Derives state from substrate via the A.3 helpers. Calls the
  same `POST /api/v1/ratify` endpoint.

Both honor the same preview → ratify → apply seam. Neither owns the
substrate; both project it. The parity doctrine makes the asymmetry
between them — CLI can drive ratify, Console cannot — the thing v1.8
exists to close.
