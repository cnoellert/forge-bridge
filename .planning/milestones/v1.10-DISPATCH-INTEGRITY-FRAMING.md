# v1.10 Dispatch Integrity — Guarding the Compile→Dispatch Seam

> **What this artifact is.** Cycle-1 writing-room framing for v1.10, drafted
> from the CR.1 close + dogfood corpus. Compact per
> [[feedback-cadence-artifacts-shrink-to-load-bearing]] — rulings + grounding
> + forward-pointers — and carries the v1.9 §consequence-analysis layer,
> because the two deliverables differ in *kind* (one is a safety guarantee,
> one is a usability best-effort) and the room must vote on that asymmetry.
>
> **What this artifact is not.** Not a roadmap, not a phase plan. Not a
> reopening of substrate authority, the ratify/assent discipline, or the CR.1
> answer-pass — all stay closed and unregressed. v1.10 touches the **routing
> between compile and dispatch**, nothing downstream of a correctly-routed
> step.
>
> **The one-line thesis.** v1.9 reattached an author at `chain_complete` and
> the dogfood proved it sits downstream of where reads actually die: **7 of 9
> failures are at the compile→dispatch seam.** The system *trusts*
> `compile_intent`'s text and then either mechanically routes it — **even into
> the mutating branch (a safety hole)** — or rigidly rejects it on ambiguity
> (a usability hole). v1.10 inserts the missing thing: **a guard/arbitration
> layer at the seam.** A read intent must be *structurally incapable* of
> reaching the mutating branch; tool-selection ambiguity must be *arbitrated*,
> not hard-stopped with leaked internals.

## The transition

v1.6 made the substrate operable; v1.7 shipped the artist surfaces; v1.8
projected authority to the Console; v1.9 stood up the read answer-pass as a
pressure instrument and **ran it** (CR.1 dogfood, 2026-06-01). The instrument
did its job: it produced a comprehension corpus, and the corpus's dominant
signal is not about answer quality at all. Every tool that *executed* returned
correct data (iteration `6`, batch groups, reels). **The legibility
bottleneck is upstream of the model's voice — in the compile and resolution
steps, before the answer-pass can fire.** That reorients the work, and the
re-aim is this milestone.

## The two failures, grounded (live reads 2026-06-01)

### ① Reads reach mutation — SAFETY (two distinct holes; DT cycle-2)

There are **two** routing paths by which a read can reach a mutation, not one.
The cycle-1 draft conflated them; DT grounding separates them, and the second
is the more severe:

- **Hole A — the commit gate (R4/R5).** `run_compile_branch` routes to the
  mutating-preview regime on a single condition: does the compiled step text
  contain a `commit` token? (`_chat_compile.py:158`, `graph_contains_commit_node`,
  def at `graph/commit.py:75/93`; `would_mutate` / `requires_ratification`
  derive mechanically at `:100-112`.) The classification is *correct*; the
  defect is upstream — the 14b model emitted `commit` for a read
  (`flame_list_batch_groups -> commit`). Result: the artist is shown a
  **"Ratify & Apply" mutation prompt** for a question. **Gated, but
  mis-routed.**
- **Hole B — the forced-tool path (R11), NO gate at all.** When the candidate
  set narrows to one tool, `_execute_forced_tool` (`handlers.py:528`) invokes
  that tool **directly** — *"The LLM router is not called on this path"* — and
  there is **no commit gate, no preview, no ratify** on it. R11
  (`"duration in frames"`) mis-selected `flame_set_start_frames` (a mutation)
  and the forced path **executed it directly**; it failed only on a missing
  required param, not on any safety guard. Had the param been resolvable, a
  read would have **mutated host state with no ratification.** This is the
  worst case in the milestone.

**There is no guard, on either path, that a read may not reach a mutation.**
The reads-vs-mutations split — which CLAUDE.md and the v1.9 framing call
*structural* — is today enforced only for the *answer-pass* (CR.1 placed (b)
in the non-mutating branch). It is **not** enforced at *routing or forced
dispatch*. v1.10 closes both holes; Hole B (unmediated execution) is the
non-negotiable.

### ② Resolver paralysis — USABILITY

When a compiled step token matches ≠1 registered tool, the resolver
hard-stops (`_step.py:326-337`) with `tool_selection_ambiguous` and leaks an
internal hint to the artist (*"Step matched 4 tools… use a more specific
verb/noun (e.g. 'list versions' instead of just 'list')"*). It **already
carries the candidate list** (`:334`) — the raw material to disambiguate — but
instead of arbitrating, it dies. Three dogfood reads (path / timewarp /
duration of a shot) hit this. It is the documented
[[feedback-pre-orchestration-resolution-paralysis]] shape, live on ordinary
reads, with a strict exact-match substrate resolver downstream of an
ambiguity-prone compile.

## Recalibrated doctrine (carried, sharpened — not reopened)

- **The reads-vs-mutations split becomes enforced at the dispatch seam, not
  merely assumed in the answer-pass branch.** This is the load-bearing safety
  law of v1.10. Today the split is a property of *where the (b) code lives*;
  v1.10 makes it a property the *router enforces* — a read intent cannot be
  routed into preview/ratify/apply regardless of what the model compiled.
- **The arbitration layer is control-flow, not meaning**
  ([[feedback-orchestrator-control-flow-not-meaning]]). Selecting among
  candidate tools — or asking the operator a *structured* "did you mean A or
  B?" — is dispatch arbitration. It MUST NOT author prose, synthesize intent,
  or invent a tool not in the candidate set. The 24.4 guard stands.
- **The ① guard fails toward the read, not toward a dead-end** (Creative
  cycle-2). When the guard blocks a mis-routed read from mutating, the artist
  must not be left with a blocked-error wall — the guard should re-route to
  the *read* answer where one exists (e.g. a mis-selected `flame_set_start_frames`
  on "duration in frames" should fall back to answering the duration), or at
  minimum say plainly "I read that as a question and won't change anything."
  A safety guard that produces a new dead-end trades one bad experience for
  another. This is a design constraint on the guard, not a softening of it.
- **The cut line is unchanged** (v1.9): explain facts that exist; invent
  nothing. v1.10 adds no new model authority; if it uses the model at all
  (② disambiguation), the model *selects from a provided set*, it does not
  generate.
- **Measure-first** (this project's grain). The intervention's effect is only
  legible against a *contemporaneous* failure-shape baseline
  ([[feedback-baseline-drift-invalidates-controls]]) — re-run the dogfood's
  failing reads on current code before any fix, capture failure-shape not just
  counts ([[feedback-failure-shape-stability-as-disposition-evidence]]).

## The asymmetry the room must vote on (§consequence-analysis)

① and ② are **not the same kind of work**, and the milestone must hold them
to different bars:

| | ① read→mutating | ② resolver paralysis |
|---|---|---|
| Class | **correctness / safety** | usability |
| Worst case | Hole B: a read **executes a mutation tool directly via the forced path, no ratify at all** (Hole A: one careless ratify away) | an artist gets a confusing error and gives up |
| Bar | a **guarantee** — deterministic, tested invariant; "best-effort" is not acceptable for a safety property | **best-effort** — measurably fewer dead-ends; an arbitration can legitimately still miss |
| Trust model | must NOT depend on a second LLM pass (don't guard a safety property with a probabilistic classifier) | MAY use a bounded LLM selection among candidates (it's control-flow) |

**Consequence:** ① leads and is the milestone's non-negotiable; ② rides as the
usability win but is allowed to be imperfect. Prompt-hardening of
`build_compile_system_prompt` (the loose `commit` instruction at `:82-86`) is
**a mitigation, not the guarantee** — it reduces bad compiles probabilistically;
the guarantee must be deterministic and live below the model.

## The spine

**v1.10 inserts a deterministic guard at the compile→dispatch seam so a read
can never be routed to mutate, and arbitrates tool-selection ambiguity instead
of hard-stopping.** It is the structural correctness the pressure instrument
exposed — not a conversational-quality project. The answer-pass (CR.1) is left
intact; v1.10 simply ensures more reads *reach* it correctly-routed and
unambiguous, which is the only way the answer-pass earns its place.

The remaining CR.1 §7 items (session/project scope; answer-pass
refuse-on-missing-field + error triage; the corpus-instrument capture-seam
fix) are **not** in the v1.10 headline. The corpus-instrument fix is named as
*enabling tooling* (§Open questions) because measuring ①② honestly requires
capturing the abort/preview seams the current instrument is blind to.

## Open questions for discuss (leans given)

- **Q-DI1 — how is "read intent" determined for the ① guard?** *Lean:
  deterministic and structural — bar `commit` and any mutation tool from any
  chain (incl. the forced path) whose request did not explicitly enter a
  mutating path; do not trust a second LLM classifier for a safety property.*
  **DT cycle-2 grounding — the annotation surface we'd want does not exist
  live:** `readOnlyHint` / `destructiveHint` are present only in
  `forge_bridge/server.py`, the documented pre-Phase-5 **orphan** (not
  re-exported, not runtime-imported — CLAUDE.md), and CLAUDE.md states synth
  tools carry `readOnlyHint=False` *always*. So a deterministic mutating/read
  classification of the **live** tool surface must be **established as part of
  v1.10** — it cannot be assumed to exist. This is the milestone's first real
  build dependency. Candidate mechanisms (discuss): annotate the live registry
  mutating/read at definition; gate the tool surface offered to `compile_intent`
  by request mode; or a deny-list of mutation tools enforced at both
  `run_compile_branch` and `_execute_forced_tool`. **Whatever the mechanism, it
  must sit at BOTH routing sites (Hole A + Hole B), not just the commit gate.**
- **Q-DI2 — ② arbitration mechanism.** *Lean: extend the existing narrowing
  machinery (reachability / PR14 / PR20 at `_step.py`), add a bounded
  candidate-selection step (deterministic ranking first; one LLM selection
  among the ≤5 candidates only if needed), and NEVER leak "matched N tools."*
  Structured "did you mean?" to the operator is the fallback, not the default.
  **Creative cycle-2:** the disambiguation must not leak **tool identifiers**
  either — `flame_get_clip` vs `forge_get_shot` is the same developer-facing
  disease one layer up. If the artist must choose, the choice is in task terms,
  not tool names. The "matched N tools" cure must not reintroduce the symptom.
- **Q-DI3 — does v1.10 touch the compile prompt?** *Lean: yes, as secondary
  mitigation only* — tighten the `commit` instruction opener
  ([[feedback-rhetorical-position-as-architectural-control-surface]]) — but the
  ratifiable deliverable is the deterministic guard, not the prompt.
- **Q-DI4 — corpus-instrument capture seam.** Fold the capture-on-abort/preview
  fix in as measurement tooling, or hold it? *Lean: fold the minimal version*
  — without it the milestone cannot measure ①'s "reads no longer reach
  mutating" or ②'s "fewer dead-ends" against the corpus.
- **Q-DI5 — baseline.** Re-run the 11 dogfood reads on current `main` for a
  contemporaneous failure-shape baseline before any change. *Lean: yes,
  prerequisite.* (Daemon was torn down at CR.1 close; same stdio-held-open
  bring-up.)

## Constraints (binding)

- **Public surface frozen:** `forge_bridge.__all__` stays **19**; no new
  external libraries. **Milestone number vs package version are decoupled here**
  (Orch cycle-2): the *milestone* is `v1.10`; the *package* version stays on the
  `1.5.x` line — consistent with v1.5–v1.9 all riding package `1.4.1 → 1.5.1`.
  Naming `v1.10` does not imply a `1.10.0` package bump; discuss sets the actual
  patch/minor.
- **The reads-vs-mutations split becomes a *tested invariant* at the dispatch
  seam** — the milestone's acceptance criterion.
- **No regression of the CR.1 answer-pass** or the preview→ratify→apply path;
  the ① guard *prevents reads from entering* the mutating path, it does not
  modify the mutating path.
- **No new model authority.** Arbitration selects; it never authors.
- **Reads-only stays reads-only.** Nothing here touches `AssentRecord`,
  ratify, or apply semantics.

## Status

**Cycle-2 milestone-framing draft, 2026-06-01** (DT grounding + Creative
experience-shape + Orch synthesis folded). The thesis, the safety-vs-usability
asymmetry, and the measure-first guard all hold. Cycle-2 changes:

- **DT — ① is TWO holes, and the second is worse.** Cycle-1 conflated the
  commit-gate (Hole A, R4/R5 → preview) with the forced-tool path (Hole B,
  R11). Grounding `_execute_forced_tool` (`handlers.py:528`) shows Hole B
  executes a mutation tool **directly, no gate** — R11 ran `flame_set_start_frames`
  and failed only on a missing param. The guarantee must sit at **both**
  routing sites; Hole B (unmediated execution) is the non-negotiable. The
  asymmetry table's worst-case updated accordingly.
- **DT — the mutation-annotation surface does not exist live.** `readOnlyHint`/
  `destructiveHint` are only in the orphan `server.py`; synth tools are
  `readOnlyHint=False` always. Q-DI1's deterministic guard cannot assume an
  annotation it would need — establishing one is v1.10's first build
  dependency.
- **Creative — two constraints on the guard:** ② arbitration must not leak
  tool *identifiers* (the "matched N tools" disease one layer up); and ①'s
  guard must **fail toward the read** (re-route to the answer / say it plainly),
  not produce a new dead-end.
- **Orch — milestone# vs package-version decoupled** (`v1.10` milestone on the
  `1.5.x` package line); scope stays coherent under "the seam."

Five discuss questions carry leans, not rulings. Ready for ratify, then
v1.10-discuss (Q-DI1 mechanism is the first decision — it has the build
dependency).
