# Phase 24.5 — Consumer Projection of `orchestration_terminated`

## §0 — Banner

24.5 is the **consumer-projection** phase of the 24.x A/B/C arc — the phase that makes the architectural truth the 24.4 envelope encodes visible to the operator at the two surfaces they actually use. **No new orchestration authority, no new envelope semantics, no new transport behavior.** The 24.4 envelope is necessary; this phase makes it sufficient.

Single load-bearing invariant carried forward from 24.4 verbatim:

> **"The orchestrator may terminate, but does not impersonate the model."**

24.5 adds its own consumer-side mirror:

> **"The consumer may project, but does not synthesize."**

Together these two invariants bind the full architectural decomposition: orchestrator owns control flow without owning meaning; consumer owns presentation without owning synthesis. Neither layer crosses into the model's lane.

## §1 — Load-Bearing Diagnosis

The 24.x A/B/C decomposition closed at PR #5 merge with the architectural shift fully encoded at the substrate + transport + telemetry layers:

- **Substrate (router):** `_OrchestrationTerminated` raised at the K=2 canonical-recurrence boundary (`router.py:639-662` trigger + raise at :988 + catch at :1018).
- **Transport (handler):** Distinct JSON envelope (`handlers.py:879-895`) carrying `stop_reason="orchestration_terminated"` + `termination.{status, trigger, reason, iterations, accumulated_results}` + full `messages` + `tool_trace`. SSE path emits `event: orchestration_terminated` after the K-th `event: message` (handler.py:898-1090).
- **Telemetry (logs):** `status=orchestration_terminated trigger=k_fold_canonical k_count=2 result_hash=...` log line at `router.py:982`.

**Three-surface coherence achieved at the architecture; zero-surface coherence achieved at the operator.** Today, when the canonical "What are the clips on Reel 1" probe fires against the merged 24.4 code on either consumer surface, here is what the operator actually sees:

**CLI (`fbridge chat`)** — `forge_bridge/cli/chat.py:35-247`:
- JSON-only consumer (no `Accept: text/event-stream` header sent; no event-stream parsing).
- Success path at lines 119-130 calls `_extract_reply(result.data)` which defensively iterates keys `("response", "reply", "text", "content", "message")` and falls back to `messages[-1].content`.
- **No knowledge of `stop_reason`.** `orchestration_terminated` falls through to the `done` branch; the operator sees the K-th tool-result content extracted as if it were the model's completion — which is *exactly* the impersonation 24.4 ruled out at the orchestrator layer, now re-introduced at the consumer layer.
- Verbose path at lines 121-129 writes `[chat] elapsed=X.XXs attempts=A/B model=M provider=P tools=N (Xs)` — does not mention orchestration provenance, does not mention trigger, does not mention iterations.

**Console UI (`/ui/` chat view)** — `forge_bridge/console/templates/chat/panel.html` + `forge_bridge/console/static/forge-chat.js`:
- JSON-only consumer (Alpine.js + `fetch()` POST; SSE never wired up despite Phase 24.3 server-side support).
- Rendering branches on `msg.role ∈ {user, assistant, tool}` (forge-chat.js:76-82); CSS modifier classes `.chat-message--user / --assistant / --tool`.
- **No knowledge of `stop_reason`.** When `orchestration_terminated` returns, the consumer follows the same path as a `done` response — the final assistant turn renders with `.chat-message--assistant` chrome, indistinguishable from a model-decided completion.

**The architectural decomposition is invisible at the only surfaces operators touch.** The 24.4 envelope is real; the operator-visible projection of that envelope is not. Without consumer projection, the architectural shift is real-but-illegible. With consumer projection, the operator can distinguish "the model decided we're done" from "the orchestrator decided we're done" from "transport failed" — three taxa at the architecture, three taxa at the operator surface.

## §2 — Core Goal

Make `orchestration_terminated` **legible and truthful** to operators at the two consumer surfaces (CLI `fbridge chat` + Console UI `/ui/` chat view) without violating:

1. **The 24.4 invariant:** *"The orchestrator may terminate, but does not impersonate the model."* — preserved by NOT introducing orchestrator-side synthesis at 24.5.
2. **The 24.5 invariant (introduced here):** *"The consumer may project, but does not synthesize."* — preserved by rendering the envelope verbatim, not paraphrasing, summarizing, or smoothing it into model-completion-shaped output.

Both invariants together close the architectural loop: orchestrator stays in control-flow lane; consumer stays in presentation lane; meaning stays at the model.

## §4 — Consumer Contract (CONVERGED)

When a consumer receives a JSON response from `POST /api/v1/chat` with `stop_reason="orchestration_terminated"`, both surfaces (CLI + Console) MUST surface these five facts:

| # | Fact | Source field | Rendering requirement |
|---|------|--------------|----------------------|
| 1 | This was a **policy-decided termination**, not a model completion | `stop_reason == "orchestration_terminated"` AND/OR `termination.status == "orchestration_terminated"` | Visually distinct from the `done` (model-decided) path; explicit prose or label naming "orchestration termination" / "policy termination" — not "completion" |
| 2 | The **trigger** that fired | `termination.trigger` (currently `"k_fold_canonical"`) | Rendered verbatim; future trigger names get the same render path without code change (per 24.4 framing §5 archaeological-naming convention) |
| 3 | The **reason** (human-readable explanation of what triggered) | `termination.reason` (e.g. "Tool X dispatched successfully K times with identical canonical arguments and identical canonical result. Loop terminated by orchestration policy.") | Verbatim; the reason string is operator-authored at the orchestrator and consumers do NOT paraphrase it |
| 4 | The **iteration count** at which termination fired | `termination.iterations` | Rendered as a numeric value with context (e.g. "after 4 iterations") |
| 5 | The **accumulated successful results** (specifically the K-th entry that fired the trigger; ideally the full sequence) | `termination.accumulated_results[]` where each entry is `{tool_name, args_hash, result_hash, content, iter}` | At minimum: the K-th entry rendered clearly (operator can see "the canonical result that repeated"). Ideally: the full ordered list showing the recurrence. Console reuses collapsed-`<details>` chrome (parity with existing `.chat-message--tool`). |

**What consumers MUST NOT do** (the §10 anti-scope binding made concrete at the contract level):

- ❌ Treat the K-th `accumulated_results[]` entry's `content` as if it were an assistant turn (this is exactly the impersonation 24.4 ruled out at the orchestrator, re-introduced at the consumer)
- ❌ Synthesize a "summary" or "friendly completion message" from the envelope
- ❌ Re-render the termination as `.chat-message--assistant` or any role chrome that signals "model finished"
- ❌ Hide the trigger / reason / iterations behind a "details" toggle that defaults to closed (`<details>` is fine for the `accumulated_results[]` list; the trigger/reason/iterations stay always-visible)
- ❌ Paraphrase the `termination.reason` string
- ❌ Inspect `messages[-1].content` and render it as the "answer" (`_extract_reply` fallback path at `cli/chat.py` — must be guarded against the `orchestration_terminated` envelope shape)

## §7 — Per-Layer Success Criteria

Per `[[feedback-distinct-success-criteria-per-adjacent-layer]]` — each consumer surface has its own success criterion attached to its native concern; no layer's win is laundered into the other's. Cross-surface parity is its own criterion.

### §7.1 — L_CLI (CLI operator-visibility)

A non-author operator running `fbridge chat "What are the clips on Reel 1"` against a daemon that fires the K=2 trigger can answer all of these from stderr/stdout output alone, without inspecting JSON, logs, or memory cursors:

1. ✅ What happened (the conversation ended)
2. ✅ Why it ended (orchestration termination, NOT model completion)
3. ✅ Which authority fired (orchestrator, named explicitly)
4. ✅ What canonical result repeated (the K-th `accumulated_results[]` entry surfaced; ideally the full sequence)
5. ✅ How many iterations the loop ran (`termination.iterations`)

Falsification test: if any of the five facts requires `--verbose`, `--json`, log inspection, or memory-cursor lookup to surface, L_CLI has failed.

### §7.2 — L_CONSOLE (Console operator-visibility)

A non-author operator viewing the same canonical query result in the Console UI chat view can answer the same five questions from the rendered chat panel alone, without opening browser devtools, viewing raw JSON, or inspecting logs.

Visual distinction test: at-a-glance (1-2 second scan), the operator can identify "this ended by orchestration termination" vs. "this ended by model completion" by visual chrome alone, without reading content.

Falsification test: if any of the five facts requires hovering, expanding a collapsed-by-default section that hides primary information, or switching tabs, L_CONSOLE has failed.

(`<details>` for the `accumulated_results[]` array is allowed because the *list* is secondary detail; the *fact that termination occurred* + trigger + reason + iterations stays always-visible.)

### §7.3 — L_PARITY (cross-surface contract parity)

Both surfaces render the same five facts from the same envelope. Verified by comparing the canonical-probe rerun bundle (§8) outputs side-by-side: every fact rendered in the CLI is also rendered in the Console, and vice versa. Any fact rendered on one surface but not the other is a parity failure.

Schema-divergence test: neither surface introduces a field reference (e.g. `termination.foo`) that does not exist in the envelope (`handlers.py:879-895`). The envelope is consumed verbatim; both surfaces are projections of the same source.

## §9 — Transport Choice (CONVERGED)

**24.5 scopes JSON envelope consumption only.** Both surfaces consume `POST /api/v1/chat` without `Accept: text/event-stream` header, receive the single-JSON-response envelope, and render the new orchestration_terminated branch off the `stop_reason` field.

**SSE consumption in CLI and Console is intentionally deferred** for two reasons:

1. **Single-perturbation discipline (per 24.3 + 24.4 precedent).** 24.5's perturbation is *consumer-side projection of an existing envelope*. Adding SSE adoption would introduce a *second* perturbation (replacing JSON-await with progressive event consumption) in the same phase — exactly the discipline failure the 24.x arc has held against repeatedly.

2. **Layer attribution clarity.** SSE consumer adoption is a Layer C extension (perceived responsiveness — operator sees the loop progressing rather than waiting for the final envelope). The K=2 termination policy fires deterministically regardless of transport — whether the consumer await JSON or stream SSE, the termination semantics are identical. Mixing the perceived-responsiveness work with the consumer-projection work would dilute both layer success criteria per `[[feedback-distinct-success-criteria-per-adjacent-layer]]`.

The SSE substrate exists (server-side). Consumer adoption is its own phase, scoped under separate framing. Recon surfaced that the Console JS has a comment mentioning history-grows but does not consume the SSE stream — this is an *honest archaeology artifact* recording the staged nature of the consumer-side work; 24.5 does not flip the switch.

## §10 — Anti-Scope Binding (Explicit)

### §10.1 — Explicitly forbidden at 24.5

The list below is the operational expression of the 24.5 invariant *"the consumer may project, but does not synthesize"*:

1. ❌ **No consumer-side synthesis.** The CLI does not generate a "natural language summary" of the termination. The Console does not insert paraphrased text. Both surfaces project the envelope's verbatim strings.
2. ❌ **No "friendly summary" / "helpful completion" / "let me explain what happened" rendering.** Operator-facing text is the verbatim `termination.reason` string + structured fact list. No prose authored at the consumer.
3. ❌ **No re-rendering the K-th tool result as the assistant turn.** The K-th `accumulated_results[]` entry's `content` is data, not assistant speech.
4. ❌ **No model-completion chrome on orchestration termination.** `.chat-message--assistant` is reserved for actual model-decided completions. `.chat-message--orchestration-terminated` (or equivalent) is the distinct chrome; mixing them is the impersonation.
5. ❌ **No envelope schema reinterpretation.** Consumer reads `stop_reason`, `termination.{status,trigger,reason,iterations,accumulated_results}` as defined at `handlers.py:879-895`. Does not infer fields, does not synthesize fields, does not normalize fields.
6. ❌ **No SSE transport adoption at consumer side** (deferred per §9).
7. ❌ **No router or handler changes.** 24.4 is closed. The substrate + transport + telemetry layers are byte-identical pre- and post-24.5. (`tests/console/test_chat_handler_sse.py` × 3 + `tests/llm/test_complete_with_tools.py` K-fold tests are the falsification suite per §7.4.)
8. ❌ **No prompt shaping, no tool-list narrowing, no system-message changes, no temperature drift.** These are 24.4 §10 forbiddens that carry forward verbatim into 24.5's binding.
9. ❌ **No "improved" `_extract_reply` fallback that handles orchestration_terminated by extracting `accumulated_results[-1].content`.** This is the most rhetorically respectable form of impersonation — "the consumer is just being smart about extracting the answer." The answer the operator needs is "the orchestrator terminated this conversation," not "here's the K-th tool result rendered as if the model said it."
10. ❌ **No new envelope fields** — if 24.5 surfaces a genuine missing field need during execution, that's a separate scoped patch (D-04), not a 24.5-internal change.

### §10.2 — The discipline

The pressure 24.5 faces is the inverse of 24.4's pressure:

- **24.4 pressure:** "since the orchestrator now decides to terminate, it should also tell the user what happened" → ruled out at orchestrator-side synthesis.
- **24.5 pressure:** "since the consumer is rendering termination, it should make the termination feel like a friendly assistant completion" → ruled out at consumer-side synthesis.

Both pressures share a single failure mode: **collapsing the architectural decomposition into a single user-facing completion experience.** The 24.x arc's whole value is keeping the decomposition legible — orchestrator decided / model decided / transport decided are different things and the operator must see them as different things.

Per `[[feedback-anti-scope-discipline-under-pressure]]`: discipline survives only when the per-layer criteria + the operating principle language + the anti-scope binding are all load-bearing together. §4 (contract) + §5 (visual distinction) + §7 (per-layer criteria) + §10 (anti-scope) hold the discipline as a system, not as four separate aspirational lists.
