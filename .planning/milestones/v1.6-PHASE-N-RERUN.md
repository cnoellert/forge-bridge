    "type": "CHAIN_WIRE_COMPATIBILITY_ERROR",

    "step_index": 1,
    "expected": [ { "kind": "list", "item_type": "IterationResult" } ],
    "actual": { "kind": "list", "item_type": "segment" },
    "message": "Step 1 cannot accept previous topology {'kind': 'list', 'item_type': 'segment'}; expected one of [{'kind': 'list', 'item_type': 'IterationResult'}]."

Note: the mismatch is item-type level — both topologies are
kind: list; the check rejected on item_type (segment vs
IterationResult). The wire contract discriminates item type, not just
topology kind. Stronger evidence than a plain manifest-vs-list rejection.
The invariant holds: collect rejects non-list[IterationResult] input
at the dispatch edge before any reconciliation runs.
